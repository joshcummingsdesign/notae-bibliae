The Greek Fathers were Church Fathers who wrote in Greek between the 2nd and 7th centuries.

- [Justin Martyr](/people/saints/greek-fathers/justin-martyr) (100–165 AD) (June 1)
- [Irenaeus of Lyon](/people/saints/greek-fathers/irenaeus-of-lyon) (130–202 AD) (June 28)
- [Athanasius of Alexandria](/people/saints/greek-fathers/athanasius-of-alexandria) (293–373 AD) (May 2)
- [Cyril of Jerusalem](/people/saints/greek-fathers/cyril-of-jerusalem) (313–386 AD) (March 18)
- [John Chrysostom](/people/saints/greek-fathers/john-chrysostom) (347–407 AD) (September 13)
- [Cyril of Alexandria](/people/saints/greek-fathers/cyril-of-alexandria) (376–444 AD) (June 27)
- [Maximus the Confessor](/people/saints/greek-fathers/maximus-the-confessor) (580–662 AD) (August 13)
- [John of Damascus](/people/saints/greek-fathers/john-of-damascus) (675–749 AD) (December 4)
