**Bishop** (c. 465 AD)<br/>

[St. Patrick of Ireland](/glossary/names#patrick), born to a Roman Christian family in Britain, was captured at sixteen by Irish raiders and enslaved for six years, during which his faith deepened; after escaping and briefly returning home, he experienced a vision calling him back to Ireland as a missionary. Ordained bishop and sent to evangelize the Irish following the failure of earlier efforts, he began his mission around 432, making his first converts, including the chieftain Dichu, and boldly preaching even before kings—famously illustrating the Trinity with a shamrock. Over many years he traveled throughout Ireland, founding churches, converting large numbers, and establishing clergy across the land. In his old age he wrote his *Confession*, giving thanks for God’s guidance in his labors, and died at Saul in 465, leaving behind a transformed Christian nation and a legacy of apostolic zeal and humility.
