**Virgin and Martyr** (c. 303 AD)

[St. Agnes of Rome](/glossary/names#agnes), about thirteen during the persecution of Diocletian, was renowned for her purity and steadfast devotion to Christ, whom she regarded as her true Bridegroom, refusing all earthly marriage despite her beauty and noble status. When her rejection of a suitor revealed her Christianity, she was brought before the authorities and urged to yield, but remained firm in her vow of chastity. Subjected to public humiliation and placed in a brothel, she was protected by an angel, and when the governor’s son rushed in, he was struck down, causing fear and uproar among the people. Condemned to death, she was placed upon a fire that did not harm her, and, after praying with joy and thanksgiving, she was finally executed by the sword, willingly offering herself to God; thus she was honored as a virgin martyr and a special patroness of purity.
