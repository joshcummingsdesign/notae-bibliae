**Deacon and Martyr** (304 AD)

[St. Vincent of Saragossa](/glossary/names#vincent), a deacon of the Church in Spain trained under Bishop Valerius, boldly confessed the Christian faith during the persecution of Diocletian and Maximian when brought before the governor Dacian. Refusing to sacrifice to the pagan gods or surrender the sacred Scriptures, he endured severe tortures with remarkable courage and serenity, even welcoming suffering for Christ’s sake. Though cast into a harsh prison, he was comforted by heavenly visions that led even his jailer to believe. After prolonged torment, he was granted a brief rest and peacefully gave up his soul in 304 AD. His body, though subjected to further indignities by his persecutors, was miraculously preserved and eventually recovered and honored by Christians, confirming his legacy as one of the most illustrious martyrs of the Spanish Church.


## Observances

- **January 22** — [Saint Vincent](/liturgy/liturgical-year/saints/saint-vincent), Deacon and Martyr
