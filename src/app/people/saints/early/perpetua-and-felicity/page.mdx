**Martyrs** (203 AD)

During the persecution under Septimius Severus around 202 AD, five catechumens at Carthage—including [Perpetua](/glossary/names#perpetua) and her slave [Felicity](/glossary/names#felicity) arrested for their faith, later joined by Saturus, their voluntary companion in imprisonment. Perpetua, a young mother of noble birth, steadfastly refused to renounce Christ despite her father’s pleas and endured harsh imprisonment, recording visions that confirmed her coming martyrdom. Felicity, who was expecting, gave birth shortly before execution after praying to be delivered in time. When brought before the judge, all boldly confessed their faith and were condemned to death by wild beasts. Refusing to wear pagan garments in the amphitheatre, they entered joyfully, encouraged one another, and faced torture with courage and composure; even after being attacked, Perpetua showed concern for modesty and aided Felicity. Their martyrdom was remembered as a triumphant witness of faith, in which, as [St. Augustine](/people/saints/latin-fathers/augustine-of-hippo) later reflected, they overcame the devil through steadfast suffering.
