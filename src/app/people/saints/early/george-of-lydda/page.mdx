**Martyr** (285 AD)

[St. George of Lydda](/glossary/names#george) was a Christian of noble origin from Cappadocia who served as a soldier under Diocletian and, during the persecution, gave away his wealth, openly confessed Christ, and was put to death for refusing to sacrifice to the pagan gods. Though later legends—most famously the story of slaying a dragon—greatly embellished his life, early evidence confirms his martyrdom and the widespread veneration of his name in the early Church. As reflected in a homily of [St. Peter Damian](/people/saints/medieval/peter-damian), George is remembered as a soldier of Christ who renounced worldly service for spiritual warfare, encouraging the faithful to imitate his courage, detachment from earthly goods, and steadfastness in the face of trial, so as to attain the glory of heaven.
