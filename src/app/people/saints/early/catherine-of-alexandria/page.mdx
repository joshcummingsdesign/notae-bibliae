## Readings

> St. Catharine [was a] virgin and martyr under the emperor Maximinus. For the confession of the Christian faith, she was cast into prison at Alexandria, and afterwards endured a long scourging with whips garnished with metal, and [was finally beheaded]. Her body was miraculously conveyed by angels to Mount Sinai, where pious veneration is paid to it by a great concourse of Christians.
>
> <cite>[*The Roman Martyrology* (1916), 363](https://archive.org/details/romanmartyrology00cathuoft/romanmartyrology00cathuoft/page/362/mode/2up)</cite>
