**Bishop and Doctor of the Church** (397 AD)

[St. Ambrose of Milan](/glossary/names#ambrose), originally a Roman official and unbaptized at the time, was unexpectedly chosen bishop of Milan in 374 by popular acclaim, which he accepted with reluctance before devoting himself fully to his episcopal duties, charitable works, and theological study. A firm defender of Nicene orthodoxy, he opposed Arian influence with courage, even refusing imperial demands to surrender churches, and inspired the faithful through preaching, hymnody, and the introduction of <Definition lang="english" anchor="antiphonal" text="antiphonal" /> singing. Known for his generosity—famously using church treasures to ransom captives—and his pastoral leadership, he also played a key role in the conversion of [St. Augustine of Hippo](/people/saints/latin-fathers/augustine-of-hippo), whom he baptized in 387. Ambrose continued to guide the Church with wisdom and humility until his death in 397, leaving a lasting legacy as a defender of the faith and a model bishop.
