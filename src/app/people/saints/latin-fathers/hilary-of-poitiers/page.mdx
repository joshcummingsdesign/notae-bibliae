**Bishop and Doctor of the Church** (368 AD)

[St. Hilary of Poitiers](/glossary/names#hilary), born into a pagan family in Gaul, converted to Christianity through study of Scripture and was elected Bishop of Poitiers around 353, despite being a married layman at the time, after which he lived in continence and became a renowned preacher who influenced figures like [St. Martin of Tours](/people/saints/early/martin-of-tours). A steadfast defender of the Nicene faith, he opposed Arianism following the condemnation of [St. Athanasius](/people/saints/greek-fathers/athanasius-of-alexandria), and after boldly confessing the faith at hostile councils, he was exiled to Phrygia by Emperor Constantius II. During exile he wrote major works such as *On the Trinity* and *On the Synods*, defending orthodox doctrine and encouraging Western bishops. Though the emperor refused to formally lift his sentence, Hilary was eventually allowed to return to Gaul, where he worked to restore doctrinal unity, convened a synod at Paris to reaffirm the consubstantiality of the Son, and continued his labors until his death in 368, after which he was honored for his holiness, writings, and miracles associated with his tomb.
