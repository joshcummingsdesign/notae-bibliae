The Latin Fathers are Church Fathers who wrote in Latin between the 2nd and 7th centuries.

- [Cyprian of Carthage](/people/saints/latin-fathers/cyprian-of-carthage) (210–258 AD) (September 16)
- [Damasus of Rome](/people/saints/latin-fathers/damasus-of-rome) (305–384 AD) (December 11)
- [Hilary of Poitiers](/people/saints/latin-fathers/hilary-of-poitiers) (310–367 AD) (January 13)
- [Ambrose of Milan](/people/saints/latin-fathers/ambrose-of-milan) (339–397 AD) (December 7)
- [Jerome of Stridon](/people/saints/latin-fathers/jerome-of-stridon) (342–420 AD) (September 30)
- [Augustine of Hippo](/people/saints/latin-fathers/augustine-of-hippo) (354–430 AD) (August 28)
- [Leo the Great](/people/saints/latin-fathers/leo-the-great) (400–461 AD) (November 10)
- [Gregory the Great](/people/saints/latin-fathers/gregory-the-great) (540–604 AD) (September 3)
- [Isidore of Seville](/people/saints/latin-fathers/isidore-of-seville) (560–636 AD) (April 4)
