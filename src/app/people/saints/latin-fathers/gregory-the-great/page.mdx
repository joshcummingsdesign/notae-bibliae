**Pope and Doctor of the Church** (604 AD)

[St. Gregory the Great](/glossary/names#gregory), born into a noble Roman family, first served as a civil magistrate before renouncing worldly life to become a monk, founding monasteries and embracing the [Benedictine rule](/meditations/the-rule-of-saint-benedict); though repeatedly drawn from the cloister for public service, he was eventually elected pope in 590 despite his reluctance. As pontiff, he labored amid political turmoil and personal sorrow, defending the Church’s unity—most notably opposing the title “Universal Bishop”—while promoting missionary work, including sending [St. Augustine of Canterbury](/people/saints/medieval/augustine-of-canterbury) to evangelize England. He also made lasting contributions to the Church’s liturgy and music, organizing chant and establishing schools for sacred song, and remained tirelessly devoted to pastoral care and administration until his death in 604, remembered for his humility, wisdom, and the enduring title he favored, “servant of the servants of God.”

## Observances

- **March 12** — [Saint Gregory the Great](/liturgy/liturgical-year/saints/saint-gregory-the-great), Pope and Doctor of the Church
