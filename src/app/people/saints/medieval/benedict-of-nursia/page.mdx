**Abbot** (543 AD)

[St. Benedict of Nursia](/glossary/names#benedict), born around 480 into a noble Roman family, withdrew from the corruption of the world as a youth to live as a hermit at Subiaco, where his holiness soon attracted disciples and led to the founding of multiple monasteries. Despite opposition and even attempts on his life, he continued to form a growing monastic community, eventually establishing the great monastery of Monte Cassino, where he overthrew lingering pagan practices and composed his influential [*Rule*](/meditations/the-rule-of-saint-benedict), which became the foundation of Western monastic life. Renowned for his wisdom, discipline, and spiritual insight, he guided his monks in prayer, labor, and obedience, shaping a model of Christian life that would renew Europe. After many years of labor, and following the death of his sister [St. Scholastica](/people/saints/medieval/scholastica-of-nursia), he died in 543, standing in prayer, leaving a lasting legacy as a spiritual father and reformer.
