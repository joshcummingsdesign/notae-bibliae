**Priest** (734 AD)

[St. Bede the Venerable](/glossary/names#bede) was sent as a child to the monastery of Wearmouth and later Jarrow, where he spent nearly his whole life in study, prayer, teaching, and writing. Ordained deacon at nineteen and priest at thirty, he became renowned for his learning and piety, composing numerous works, especially his *Ecclesiastical History of England*, the chief early source for the history of Christianity in England. His life was marked by humble industry, as he taught the monks, studied Scripture, and wrote without assistance. During his final illness, he remained joyful and prayerful, continuing to teach, chant psalms, distribute gifts, and labor over his final writings; after his last sentence had been transcribed, he died peacefully on Ascension Day while singing the Gloria Patri.
