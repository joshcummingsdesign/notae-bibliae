**Virgin** (543 AD)

[St. Scholastica of Nursia](/glossary/names#scholastica), the twin sister of [St. Benedict](/people/saints/medieval/benedict-of-nursia), consecrated herself to God from childhood and became a model for religious women, living in a monastery near Monte Cassino under her brother’s spiritual guidance. Though they met only once a year, their final meeting became her most famous episode: after a day of holy conversation, she begged Benedict to remain with her through the night, and when he refused out of obedience to his rule, she prayed, and a sudden storm prevented his departure, allowing them to continue their spiritual discourse. [St. Gregory the Great](/people/saints/latin-fathers/gregory-the-great) later explained that her prayer prevailed because she loved more deeply. Three days after their parting, Benedict saw a vision of her soul ascending to heaven in the form of a dove, confirming her sanctity and sealing her memory as a woman of profound love, prayer, and devotion.
