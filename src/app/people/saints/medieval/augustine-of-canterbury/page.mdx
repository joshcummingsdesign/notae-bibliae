**Bishop** (604 AD)

[St. Augustine of Canterbury](/glossary/names#augustine), prior of a Roman monastery under [St. Gregory the Great](/people/saints/latin-fathers/gregory-the-great), was sent with forty companions to convert the Anglo-Saxons, arriving at Ebbsfleet in Kent after a difficult journey through Gaul. Received by King Ethelbert, he and his monks settled at Canterbury, worshipping first in the ancient church of [St. Martin](/people/saints/early/martin-of-tours) and preaching with such holiness and simplicity that many Saxons embraced the faith, including the king himself. Consecrated archbishop of the English, Augustine established Canterbury as the center of the new English Church, founded churches and monasteries, and received further support and authority from Gregory to organize bishoprics throughout England. Though resisted by the older British Church and at times insulted, mocked, and driven away by pagan Saxons, he persevered in his mission until his death in 604, remembered as the apostle of the English.
