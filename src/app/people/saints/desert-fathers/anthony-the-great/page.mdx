**Abbot** (356 AD)

[St. Anthony the Great](/glossary/names#anthony) was born in Egypt and raised in a devout Christian home. After hearing Christ’s command in the Gospel, gave away his inheritance, entrusted his sister to a community of virgins, and devoted himself to a life of ascetic discipline, prayer, and manual labor. Learning from other holy men, he cultivated virtues such as humility, patience, and charity, becoming known as a “friend of God.” After years of solitude and spiritual struggle, he emerged transformed—sound in body and rich in grace—teaching, healing, and exhorting others to love Christ above all things, which led many to embrace the monastic life and fill the desert with communities under his guidance. Near the end of his life, he prepared peacefully for death, encouraging perseverance, distributing his few possessions, and requesting a secret burial, before departing with joy and hope in the resurrection.
