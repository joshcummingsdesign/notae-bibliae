**Evangelist** (c. 68 AD)

[St. Mark](/glossary/names#mark), believed to have been born of Jewish parents of the tribe of Levi, became a close companion and interpreter of [St. Peter](/people/saints/apostles/peter), assisting him in preaching the Gospel and composing the [Gospel of Mark](/bible/new-testament/gospels/mark) from Peter's teaching, likely for Gentile Christians at Rome. He is also said to have founded the Church at Aquileia before being sent to Egypt, where he established the Church of Alexandria and converted many through his preaching, miracles, and holy example, some even tracing the beginnings of monasticism to his followers. Traveling throughout Egypt and Libya, he strengthened the Christian communities and appointed pastors for the Church before suffering martyrdom at Alexandria. His memory endured throughout the Christian world through his Gospel, his liturgical legacy at Alexandria, and his witness as an Evangelist and martyr. In the Anglican tradition, his feast day, April 25th, coincides with the reciting of the Litany from the *Book of Common Prayer*, which serves to thank God and seek his blessing upon the fruits of the earth.

## Observances

- **April 25** — [Saint Mark](/liturgy/liturgical-year/saints/saint-mark), Evangelist
