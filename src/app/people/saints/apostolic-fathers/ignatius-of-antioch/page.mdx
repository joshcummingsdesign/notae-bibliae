**Bishop and Martyr** (107 AD)

[St. Ignatius of Antioch](/glossary/names#ignatius), a disciple of [St. John](/people/saints/apostles/john) and bishop appointed by [St. Peter](/people/saints/apostles/peter), led the Church of Antioch for about forty years and was known as “Theophorus,” or God-bearer, for his devotion to Christ. According to tradition, he introduced <Definition lang="english" anchor="antiphonal" text="antiphonal" /> chanting into the Antiochian Church after a vision of angels praising the Trinity. Arrested during the reign of Emperor Trajan around 107 AD, Ignatius boldly confessed Christ and was condemned to be taken to Rome and martyred by wild beasts. On his journey, he met fellow bishops such as [St. Polycarp](/people/saints/apostolic-fathers/polycarp-of-smyrna) and wrote several influential letters to various churches, expressing not only his steadfast faith but also his desire to embrace martyrdom. Upon arriving in Rome, he was executed in the amphitheatre during public games, and his death was remembered as a powerful witness of courage, humility, and complete resignation to God’s will.
