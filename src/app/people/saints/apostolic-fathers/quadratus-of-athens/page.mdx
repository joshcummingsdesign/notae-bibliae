**Bishop** (129 AD)

[St. Quadratus of Athens](/glossary/names#quadratus) was a Greek Apostolic Father and early Christian apologist, remembered as Bishop of Athens after the martyrdom of Publius. According to [Eusebius](/people/scholars/eusebius), his zeal revived the faith of the Athenian Christians whom he brought together again.

Quadratus is chiefly known for an apology addressed to Emperor Hadrian around 124 AD, written in defense of Christianity when believers were being troubled by hostile accusers. Though the work itself is mostly lost, Eusebius preserves a notable fragment in which Quadratus argues from the lasting testimony of those healed and raised by Christ, some of whom, he says, lived even into his own day.

Eusebius praised Quadratus as a man of understanding and apostolic faith, while [St. Jerome](/people/saints/latin-fathers/jerome-of-stridon) called him a disciple of the apostles. Tradition also holds that Quadratus was one of the Seventy (Luke 10:1), and he is remembered as one of the earliest defenders of the Christian faith before the Roman Empire.
