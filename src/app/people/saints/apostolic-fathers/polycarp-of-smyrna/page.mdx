**Bishop and Martyr** (167 AD)

[St. Polycarp of Smyrna](/glossary/names#polycarp), a disciple of [St. John](/people/saints/apostles/john) and bishop of Smyrna, was a revered leader of the early Church who maintained unity even amid disagreements over the date of Easter, notably with Anicetus of Rome. During the persecution under Marcus Aurelius, he refused to flee or renounce Christ, calmly surrendering himself, praying for the Church, and boldly confessing his faith before the authorities. When urged to save his life, he declared his lifelong fidelity to Christ and willingly accepted martyrdom. Condemned to be burned, he endured the flames unharmed in a miraculous sign, and was ultimately slain by the sword, sealing his witness; afterward, his remains were reverently preserved by the faithful, who honored his memory as a steadfast bishop and martyr of the apostolic age.
