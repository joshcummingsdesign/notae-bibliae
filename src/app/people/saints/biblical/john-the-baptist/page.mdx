**c. 30 AD**

[St. John the Baptist](/glossary/names#john), the son of the priest Zacharias and Elizabeth, was miraculously conceived in their old age after the angel Gabriel announced that he would prepare the way of the Lord in the spirit and power of Elijah. Consecrated as a Nazirite from birth, he lived an ascetic life in the wilderness until he emerged preaching repentance and baptizing in the Jordan, calling all people to bear fruits worthy of repentance in preparation for the coming Messiah. He baptized Jesus, witnessed the descent of the Holy Spirit and the Father's voice from heaven, and boldly proclaimed Christ as "the Lamb of God" and the Son of God. Fearlessly rebuking Herod Antipas for his unlawful marriage to Herodias, John was imprisoned at the castle of Machaerus and, at Herodias's urging through her daughter Salome, was beheaded after Herod's birthday feast. He is remembered as the forerunner of Christ, the last and greatest of the Old Testament prophets, and the faithful witness who prepared the way for the coming of the Lord.
