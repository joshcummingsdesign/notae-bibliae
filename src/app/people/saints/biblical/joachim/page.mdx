**Father of the Blessed Virgin Mary** (c. 18 BC)

[St. Joachim](/glossary/names#joachim) was a righteous descendant of David and the husband of [St. Anne](/people/saints/biblical/anne), chosen by God to be the father of the [Blessed Virgin Mary](/people/saints/biblical/blessed-virgin-mary). Though Joachim and Anne long endured the sorrow and reproach of childlessness, they remained steadfast in prayer, charity, and devotion until, by divine promise, they were granted a daughter who would become the Mother of God. [St. John of Damascus](/people/saints/greek-fathers/john-of-damascus) presents Mary as the fulfillment of the promises made to David and emphasizes her holiness from childhood, describing her upbringing in the temple and her preparation to receive Christ. Joachim and Anne are therefore praised for their faithfulness and holy manner of life, through which they became the earthly ancestors of the Incarnate Lord and instruments in God's plan of salvation.

## Observances

- **March 20** — [Saint Joachim](/liturgy/liturgical-year/saints/saint-joachim), Father of the Blessed Virgin Mary
