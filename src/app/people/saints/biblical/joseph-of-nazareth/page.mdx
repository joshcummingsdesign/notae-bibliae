**Spouse of the Blessed Virgin Mary** (before 30 AD)

[St. Joseph of Nazareth](/glossary/names#joseph), the spouse of the [Blessed Virgin Mary](/people/saints/biblical/blessed-virgin-mary), is honored as the faithful guardian of both Mary and the Christ Child, entrusted with a role of unique dignity in the divine plan. As reflected in the writings of [St. Bernard of Clairvaux](/people/saints/medieval/bernard-of-clairvaux), he is likened to the patriarch Joseph of old, sharing in fidelity, purity, and participation in God’s hidden purposes. Of the house of David yet living in humble poverty as a carpenter, Joseph is described in the Gospels as a just man, who, upon learning of Mary’s mysterious pregnancy, resolved to act mercifully until reassured by divine revelation. In obedience to angelic guidance, he protected his family—fleeing into Egypt to escape Herod the Great and later settling in Nazareth—while faithfully fulfilling the duties of the Law and raising Jesus in quiet devotion. Though little is recorded of his later life, he is traditionally believed to have died before Christ’s public ministry, leaving a legacy of humility, obedience, and steadfast care in the service of God’s saving work.

- **March 19** — [Saint Joseph](/liturgy/liturgical-year/saints/saint-joseph), Spouse of the Blessed Virgin Mary
