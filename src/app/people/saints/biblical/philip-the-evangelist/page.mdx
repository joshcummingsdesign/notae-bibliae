**Deacon** (1st Century)

[St. Philip the Evangelist](/glossary/names#philip) was one of the first seven deacons chosen by the apostles to care for the poor of the Christian community in Jerusalem (Acts 6:3-5), upon whom the apostles laid their hands (Acts 6:6). After the persecution that followed the death of St. Stephen, Philip went to Samaria, where he preached Christ, worked miracles, and baptized many converts (Acts 8:5-13), among whom was Simon the Sorcerer (Acts 8:9-13).

From Samaria he was directed by an angel of the Lord to go toward Gaza (Acts 8:26), where he encountered and instructed the Ethiopian eunuch, a servant of Candace, queen of Ethiopia (Acts 8:27). Having preached Jesus to him and baptized him (Acts 8:35-38), Philip became associated with an event traditionally regarded as marking the beginning of Christianity in Ethiopia.

Afterward, Philip was miraculously carried away and found at Azotus (Acts 8:39-40), from which he continued preaching throughout the coastal region until he came to Caesarea (Acts 8:40). There he settled and became known for his hospitality and devotion to the Gospel, and it was there that St. Paul the Apostle later stayed with him during his journeys (Acts 21:8).

Philip lived at Caesarea with his four daughters, who possessed the gift of prophecy (Acts 21:9), and Christian tradition records that their memory long remained honored in that city.
