**Apostle** (1st century AD)

[St. Philip](/glossary/names#philip), from Bethsaida near the Sea of Galilee, the city of Saints [Peter](/people/saints/apostles/peter) and [Andrew](/people/saints/apostles/andrew) (John 1:44) and was called among the original twelve apostles (Matt. 10:3). He was also connected with [St. Bartholomew](/people/saints/apostles/bartholomew), whom he brought to Jesus (John 1:45), and appears several times in the Gospel narrative: at the feeding of the five thousand (John 6:5-7), when he and Andrew brought to Jesus the Greeks who wished to see him after the Triumphal Entry (John 12:20-22), and most memorably when he said, “Lord, show us the Father, and it is enough for us” (John 14:8). After the Resurrection, tradition says that Philip preached throughout Asia Minor, converting many, working miracles, establishing churches, and appointing pastors. At Hierapolis in Phrygia, he opposed pagan idolatry and was eventually seized, scourged, and martyred, being remembered as a faithful apostle and witness of Christ.
