**Apostle** (1st century AD)

[St. James the Less](/glossary/names#james), also called James the Younger (Mark 15:40), James the Lord's brother (Gal. 1:19), and James the Just, was one of the twelve apostles (Matt. 10:3). He is traditionally understood as the son of [Cleopas](/glossary/names#cleopas), one of the men who encountered Jesus on the road to Emmaus (Luke 24:18), and of Mary, the sister of St. Joseph (John 19:25), making him a kinsman of the Lord and the brother of Saints [Jude](/people/saints/apostles/jude), [Matthew](/people/saints/apostles/matthew), and [Simon](/people/saints/apostles/simon) (Matt. 9:9; Mark 2:14; Matt. 10:3; Matt. 13:55). After the Resurrection, Christ appeared specially to James (1 Cor. 15:7), who later became bishop of Jerusalem and a chief leader of the apostolic Church, receiving [St. Paul](/people/saints/apostles/paul) after his conversion (Gal. 1:18-19), being named by [St. Peter](/people/saints/apostles/peter) after his escape from prison (Acts 12:17), and playing a central role at the Council of Jerusalem (Acts 15:13-21). Renowned for his righteousness and faithful pastoral care, he brought many Jews to faith in Christ and authored the Epistle of James. Around 62 AD, his enemies placed him on the pinnacle of the temple to speak against Christ, but he instead boldly confessed that Jesus sits at the right hand of God and will come again in glory. Enraged, they threw him down, stoned him as he prayed for them, and finally killed him with a fuller's club.
