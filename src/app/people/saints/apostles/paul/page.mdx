**Apostle** (66 AD)

[St. Paul](/glossary/names#paul) was a Pharisee of the tribe of Benjamin, born in Tarsus and trained in Jerusalem under Gamaliel. He zealously persecuted Christians until a dramatic encounter with Christ on the road to Damascus left him blinded, converted, and baptized. After a period of preparation, he boldly proclaimed the faith, escaping persecution and being received by the apostles through the help of [St. Barnabas](/people/saints/apostles/barnabas). From Antioch he began his missionary labors, traveling widely to preach the Gospel—especially among the Gentiles—while enduring hardships, opposition, and repeated threats to his life. Arrested in Jerusalem and sent to Rome, he continued to preach even in captivity, writing numerous epistles to the churches throughout the Roman world. After further labors he was again imprisoned and ultimately martyred at Rome, remaining steadfast in faith to the end.
