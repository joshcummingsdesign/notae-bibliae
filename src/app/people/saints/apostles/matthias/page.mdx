**Apostle** (after 60 AD)

[Matthias](/glossary/names#matthew) the Apostle, traditionally believed to have been among the seventy disciples who followed Christ throughout his public ministry, was chosen by lot to replace Judas Iscariot after the Ascension, so that the apostolic college might again be complete. As described in the Book of Acts, the apostles sought one who had witnessed Christ's ministry and resurrection, entrusting the final choice to God through prayer and casting lots. After receiving the gift of the Holy Spirit, Matthias devoted himself to preaching the Gospel, first in Judaea and later, according to tradition, in regions farther east, enduring hardship and persecution while bringing many to the faith. Ancient tradition holds that he ultimately suffered martyrdom—some accounts saying by crucifixion—and his relics were later claimed by both Rome and Trier, leaving him honored as a faithful witness to the resurrection of Christ.
