**Apostle** (65 AD)

[St. Peter](/glossary/names#peter), originally named [Simon](/glossary/names#simon), was a fisherman from Bethsaida and the brother of [St. Andrew](/people/saints/apostles/andrew). First a disciple of [St. John the Baptist](/people/saints/biblical/john-the-baptist), he was called by Christ to become a fisher of men and soon emerged as the chief among the Twelve, serving as their spokesman and receiving from Christ the commission to feed his sheep after his threefold confession of love following the Resurrection. Tradition holds that Saints Peter and [Paul](/people/saints/apostles/paul) later established the church in Rome, where they faithfully preached the Gospel. There Peter opposed false teachers such as Simon Magus and helped strengthen the Christian community. During the persecution under Emperor Nero, Peter was condemned to crucifixion and, considering himself unworthy to die in the same manner as his Lord, requested to be crucified upside down. His tomb on the Vatican Hill became one of the most venerated sites in Christendom and the location of the great basilica dedicated in his honor.
