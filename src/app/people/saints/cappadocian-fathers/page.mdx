<Initial text="Cappadocian Fathers" />

The Cappadocian Fathers were a 4th-century monastic family in the the Cappadocia region in modern-day Turkey, which was an early site of Christian activity, with several missions by [St. Paul](/people/saints/apostles/paul) in this region. They advanced the development of early Christian theology.

- [Gregory of Nazianzus](/people/saints/cappadocian-fathers/gregory-of-nazianzus) (329–390 AD) (January 2)
- [Basil the Great](/people/saints/cappadocian-fathers/basil-the-great) (330–379 AD) (January 2)
- [Gregory of Nyssa](/people/saints/cappadocian-fathers/gregory-of-nyssa) (335–394 AD) (January 10)
