## Syriac Fathers

Early Church Fathers who wrote in Syriac between the 3rd and 8th centuries. Syriac is an Aramaic dialect that emerged during the first century AD. During the Early Christian period, it became the main literary language of various Aramaic-speaking Christian communities in the historical region of Ancient Syria and throughout the Near East. Many important early Christian Syriac texts have been preserved, and Syriac is still used by Syrian Christians as a liturgical language to this day.

- [Aphrahat the Persian](/people/saints/syriac-fathers/aphrahat-the-persian) (270–345 AD) (January 29)
- [Ephrem the Syrian](/people/saints/syriac-fathers/ephrem-the-syrian) (306–379) (June 9)
- [Jacob of Serugh](/people/saints/syriac-fathers/jacob-of-serugh) (451–521) (November 29)
- [Isaac of Nineveh](/people/saints/syriac-fathers/isaac-of-nineveh) (613–700 AD) (January 28)

## Desert Fathers

Early monastics living in the Egyptian desert in the 4th century who are credited with beginning the practice of monasticism, and codifying many of its features. One such example is that they are said to be the first to use knotted prayer ropes to keep track of praying through all 150 psalms.

- [Paul of Thebes](/people/saints/desert-fathers/paul-of-thebes) (227–341 AD) (January 15)
- [Anthony the Great](/people/saints/desert-fathers/anthony-the-great) (251–356 AD) (January 17)
- [Pachomius the Great](/people/saints/desert-fathers/pachomius-the-great) (290–348 AD) (May 9)
