<Initial text="The Transfiguration of Christ" />

**August 6**<br/>
Forty Days Before Holy Cross<br/>
*Feast*

The word transfiguration comes from the Latin *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="transfiguratio" text="transfiguratio" />* which means "a major change in appearance or form", and refers to an experience of momentary divine radiance in which Jesus becomes radiant in glory upon a mountain, traditionally identified as Mount Tabor, though the location is debated. Although Scripture does not tell us the date of the Transfiguration, is has been traditionally observed on August 6th, 40 days before the feast of [The Exaltation of the Holy Cross](/liturgy/liturgical-year/seasons/trinitytide/holy-cross). The 40 days between Transfiguration and the Cross mirrors a spiritual journey—a movement from glory to suffering, from the mount of divine light (Tabor) to the mount of sacrifice (Calvary).
