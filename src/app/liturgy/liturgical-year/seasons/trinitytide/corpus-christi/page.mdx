**Thursday After Trinity Sunday**

<Definition lang="latin" anchor="corpus-christi" text="Corpus Christi" /> celebrates the Real Presence of Christ in the Eucharist and the centrality of the Lord's Supper in the life of the Church. The feast focuses on thanksgiving for Christ's self-giving in the bread and wine and invites the faithful to reflect on the spiritual nourishment and unity found in the Sacrament. The feast of Corpus Christi was proposed by [St. Thomas Aquinas](/people/saints/medieval/thomas-aquinas) in order to create a feast focused solely on the Holy Eucharist, and it was added to the calendar in 1264 by Pope Urban IV. It is celebrated on the Thursday after Trinity Sunday, as it it meant to be a liturgical echo to [Maundy Thursday](/liturgy/liturgical-year/seasons/lent/passiontide/maundy-thursday).
