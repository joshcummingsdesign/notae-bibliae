**The Three Days Before Ascension Day**<br/>
*Lesser Observance*

The <Definition lang="latin" anchor="rogatio" text="Rogation Days" /> are the three days leading up to Ascension Day. The Sunday before—the Sixth Sunday of Easter—is known as Rogation Sunday. The Rogation Days originated in the 5th century as days of fasting and prayer to protect the natural environment. They also help to prepare our hearts and minds for the celebration of Christ's Ascension. In the Anglican tradition, these are the days on which the Litany from the *Book of Common Prayer* is recited as an act of intercession. Processions and the blessing of crops are also common features of Rogation Days.
