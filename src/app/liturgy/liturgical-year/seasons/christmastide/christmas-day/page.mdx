<Initial text="Christmas Day" />

**December 25**<br/>
*Principal Feast*

<Definition lang="english" anchor="christmas" text="Christmas Day" />, aslo called the Nativity of the Lord, is the celebration of the birth of Jesus Christ, the Son of God. Tradition holds that Christ was conceived by the Holy Spirit on March 25th, which coincides with the day he was crucified.[^1] Thus, the Annunciation is celebrated on March 25th, and Christmas is celebrated on December 25th. Interestingly, if Zechariah entered the temple to burn incense on <Definition lang="hebrew" anchor="yom-kippur" text="Yom Kippur" /> in September (Luke 1:8-9), and Jesus was conceived by the Holy Spirit six months later on Passover in March (Luke 1:26-27), nine months after that would be December.

[^1]: [St. Augustine](/people/saints/latin-fathers/augustine-of-hippo), [*On the Trinity*](https://www.newadvent.org/fathers/130104.htm), 4.9
