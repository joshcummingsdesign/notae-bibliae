**December 28**<br/>
*Feast*

The feast of the Holy Innocents commemorates the male children two and under who were killed by Herod the Great. It is also sometimes called Childermas. Herod the Great summoned the wise men to go to Bethlehem to find the Messianic King. On their way to Bethlehem, the wise men saw the star where Jesus was, and followed it. The wise men saw Jesus, fell down and worshiped, and offered him gifts of gold, frankincense and myrrh. The wise men were warned in a dream not to return to Herod, so they returned home a different way. An angel of the Lord appeared to Joseph in a dream and told him to take Mary and Jesus and flee to Egypt as Herod was out to destroy Jesus. They fled to Egypt, which coincides with Hosea's prophecy, "Out of Egypt I called my son" (Hos. 11:1). Herod realized that the wise men had not returned, so he killed all the male children two and under in Bethlehem. This coincides with Jeremiah's prophecy that "Rachel is weeping for her children" (Jer. 31:15).
