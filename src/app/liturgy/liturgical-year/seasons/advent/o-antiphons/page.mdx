**Last Eight Nights of Advent**

The O Antiphons are a series of plainchant antiphons which are sung before and after the [Magnificat](/liturgy/music/chants/magnificat) the last seven nights of Advent at [Vespers](/glossary/liturgical-terms#vespers). In the 6th century, [Boethius](/people/scholars/boethius) referred to the text in his *Consolation of Philosophy*, and manuscripts containing all seven antiphons date to the 11th century. As the name implies, each antiphon begins with the exclamation "O", and is addressed to Christ under one of his scriptural titles. They are a magnificent theology that uses ancient biblical imagery drawn from the messianic hopes of the Old Testament to proclaim the coming of Christ as the fulfillment not only of Old Testament hopes, but of present ones as well. The medieval English <Definition lang="latin" anchor="usus" text="use" /> added the eighth antiphon for the [Blessed Virgin Mary](/people/saints/biblical/blessed-virgin-mary) and moved the O Antiphons to start one day earlier.

- **December 16** — [O Sapientia](/liturgy/music/chants/o-sapientia)
- **December 17** — [O Adonai](/liturgy/music/chants/o-adonai)
- **December 18** — [O Radix Jesse](/liturgy/music/chants/o-radix-jesse)
- **December 19** — [O Clavis David](/liturgy/music/chants/o-clavis-david)
- **December 20** — [O Oriens](/liturgy/music/chants/o-oriens)
- **December 21** — [O Rex gentium](/liturgy/music/chants/o-rex-gentium)
- **December 22** — [O Emmanuel](/liturgy/music/chants/o-emmanuel)
- **December 23** — [O Virgo virginum](/liturgy/music/chants/o-virgo-virginum)
