This calendar uses the following ranking system, which is a simplified harmonization of the [Use of Sarum](/liturgy/rites/sarum-rite), the 1928 American *Book of Common Prayer*, the [General Roman Calendar](/glossary/liturgical-terms#general-roman-calendar) (1969), and the Church of England's [*Common Worship*](/glossary/liturgical-terms#common-worship) (2000). This system is used not only for simplicity and practicality, but because it attempts to reflect the broader Western liturgical tradition.

  - Septuagesima

  - Annunciation of the Lord
  - Easter Day

  - Trinity Sunday
  - All Saints' Day

  - Ash Wednesday
  - Holy Week
  - All Souls' Day

### Feasts
  - Circumcision and Holy Name of Jesus
  - Presentation of the Lord (Purification)
  - Marian Feasts
  - Corpus Christi
  - Transfiguration of the Lord
  - Holy Cross Day
  - Apostles and Evangelists
  - Biblical Martyrs

### Lesser Observances
  - Ember Days
  - Rogation Days

### Commemorations
  - Saints
  - Martyrs
  - Church Fathers

### Ordinary Sundays
  - All other Sundays of the liturgical year

## Precedence

- Principal Sundays and Principal Feasts have the highest level of precedence, all other observances are omitted.
- Principal Feasts have Vigils that begin at Evening Prayer on the preceding day.
- All the days in the [Octave of Easter](/glossary/liturgical-terms#octave) are ranked as Principal Feasts.
- All the days in Whitsuntide are ranked as Principal Feasts, except for Ember Days.
- If a Feast falls on a higher-ranking day, the Feast is transferred to the next available date.
- If a Feast falls on an Ordinary Sunday, the Feast takes precedence, but the [collect](/glossary/liturgical-terms#collect) for the Sunday is said after the collect for the Feast.
- If a Commemoration falls on a higher-ranking day, the Commemoration is omitted.
- If a Commemoration falls on an Ordinary Sunday, the Sunday takes precedence, but the collect for the Commemoration is said after the collect for the Sunday.
