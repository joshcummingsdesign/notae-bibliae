The following is a harmonization of the ranking systems used in the 1928 American *Book of Common Prayer* and the [Use of Sarum](/liturgy/rites/sarum-rite). This ranking system is used not only for simplicity and practicality, but because this calendar supplements the Prayer Book's observances with those from the Use of Sarum in order to preserve the Prayer Book's rich liturgical heritage.

## Examples

### Principal Sundays
  - Sundays in Advent
  - Septuagesima
  - Sexagesima
  - Quinquagesima
  - Sundays in Lent
  - Rogation Sunday
  - Sunday After Ascension Day

### Principal Feasts
  - Christmas Day
  - The Epiphany
  - Annunciation of the Lord
  - Octave of Easter
  - Ascension Day
  - Whitsuntide, except Ember Days
  - Trinity Sunday
  - All Saints' Day

### Greater Observances
  - Ash Wednesday
  - Holy Week
  - All Souls' Day

### Feasts
  - Circumcision and Holy Name of Jesus
  - Presentation of the Lord (Purification)
  - Marian Feasts
  - Corpus Christi
  - Transfiguration of the Lord
  - Holy Cross Day
  - Apostles and Evangelists
  - Biblical Martyrs

### Lesser Observances
  - Ember Days
  - Rogation Days

### Commemorations
  - Saints
  - Martyrs
  - Church Fathers

### Ordinary Sundays
  - All other Sundays of the liturgical year

## Precedence

- If a Principal Feast falls on a Principal Sunday, the Principal Feast is transferred to the next available date.
- If a Principal Feast falls on an Ordinary Sunday, the Ordinary Sunday is omitted.
- If a Feast falls on a higher-ranking day, the Feast is transferred to the next available date.
- If a Feast falls on an Ordinary Sunday, the Feast takes precedence, but the [collect](/glossary/liturgical-terms#collect) for the Sunday is said after the collect for the Feast.
- If a Commemoration falls on a higher-ranking day, the Commemoration is omitted.
- If a Commemoration falls on an Ordinary Sunday, the Sunday takes precedence, but the collect for the Commemoration is said after the collect for the Sunday.
