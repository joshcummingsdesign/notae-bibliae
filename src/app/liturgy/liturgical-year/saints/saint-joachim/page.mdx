<Initial text="Saint Joachim" />

**March 20**<br/>
Father of the Blessed Virgin Mary (c. 18 BC)<br/>
*Commemoration*

## Morning Prayer

*<Red text="✠" /> From a homily by [St. John of Damascus](/people/saints/greek-fathers/john-of-damascus):* [[The Blessed Virgin Mary](/people/saints/biblical/blessed-virgin-mary)]—being pre-ordained by the eternal prescient counsel of God and imaged forth and proclaimed in diverse images and discourses of the prophets through the Holy Spirit—sprang at the pre-determined time from the root of David, according to the promises that were made to him. "For the Lord hath sworn, he saith in truth to David, he will not turn from it: of the fruit of thy body will I set upon thy throne" (Ps. 132:11). And again, "Once have I sworn by my holiness, that I will not lie unto David. His seed shall endure for ever, and his throne as the sun before me. It shall be established forever as the moon, and as a faithful witness in heaven" (Ps. 89:35-37). And Isaiah says: "And there shall come out a rod out of the stem of Jesse and a branch shall grow out of his roots" (Isa. 11:1).

Born then of the line of Nathan, the son of David, Levi begot ... Panther; Panther begot Barpanther; ... [and] Barpanther begot [Joachim](/people/saints/biblical/joachim) ... Joachim then took to wife that revered and praiseworthy woman, [Anne](/people/saints/biblical/anne), [also called Anna]. But just as the earlier Anna (cf. 1 Sam. 1:2), who was barren, bore Samuel by prayer and by promise, so also this Anna by supplication and promise from God bore the Mother of God in order that she might not even in this be behind the matrons of fame. Accordingly it was grace ... that bore Our Lady—for she became truly the Lady of all created things in becoming the Mother of the Creator. [She was born in the house of Joachim by the Sheep Gate (cf. John 5:2)], and was brought up to the temple. Then, planted in the House of God and [nourished] by the Spirit like a fruitful olive tree, she became the home of every virtue, turning her mind away from every secular and carnal desire, and thus keeping her soul as well as her body [pure], as was [fitting] for her who was to receive God into her bosom—for as he is holy, he finds rest among the holy (cf. Ps. 18:25-26). Thus, therefore, she strove after holiness, and was declared a holy and wonderful temple fit for the most high God.

Moreover, since the enemy of our salvation was keeping a watchful eye on virgins, ... the maiden [was] given in marriage to [Joseph](/people/saints/biblical/joseph-of-nazareth), [the son of David,] by the priests ... But when the fullness of time [had] come, the messenger of the Lord was sent to her, with the good news of our Lord's conception. And thus she conceived the Son of God, the hypostatic power of the Father, "not of the will of the flesh nor of the will of man" (John 1:13), ... but by the good pleasure of the Father and [the working] of the Holy Spirit.[^1]

## Evening Prayer

*<Red text="✠" /> From a homily by St. John of Damascus:* St. Anne was to be the mother of the Virgin Mother of God, and hence nature did not dare to anticipate the flowering of grace. Thus nature remained fruitless, until grace produced its fruit. For she who was to be born had to be a first born daughter, since she would be the mother of the firstborn of all creation, "in whom all things are held together" (Col. 1:17). Saints Joachim and Anne, how blessed a couple! All creation is indebted to you. For at your hands the Creator was offered a gift excelling all other gifts: a venerable mother, who alone was worthy of him.

And so rejoice, St. Anne, that "you were barren and have not borne children; break forth into shouts, you who have not given birth" (Isa. 54:1). Rejoice, Joachim, because from your daughter "a child is born for us, a son is given us, whose name is Messenger of great counsel and [salvation unto the world], Mighty God" (cf. Isa. 9:6). ... For this child is God.

Saints Joachim and Anne, how blessed and spotless a couple! You will be known by the fruit you have borne, as the Lord says: "By their fruits you will know them" (Matt. 7:16). The conduct of your life pleased God and was worthy of your daughter. For by the chaste and holy life you led together, you have fashioned a jewel of virginity.

Saints Joachim and Anne, how virtuous a couple! While safeguarding the temperance proper to the law of nature, you attained with God's help something which transcends nature in giving the world the Virgin Mother of God as your daughter. While leading a devout and holy life in your human nature, you gave birth to a daughter nobler than the angels ... Daughter of Adam and Mother of God ... Blessed are the arms that carried you ... Rejoice in God, all the earth. Sing! Exult and sing hymns. Raise your voice! Raise it and be not afraid.[^2]
