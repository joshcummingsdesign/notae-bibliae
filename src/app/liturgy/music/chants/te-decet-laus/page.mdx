## History

A hymn which appears in the 4th-century *Apostolic Constitutions*. The Latin version was in existence by the 6th century, when it was prescribed for use at [Matins](/glossary/liturgical-terms#matins) in the [*Rule of Saint Benedict*](/people/saints/medieval/benedict-of-nursia).[^1]  The words are an allusion to Ps. 65:1.

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b Te decet laus, te decet hymnus,
|  \b tibi gloria Deo Patri et Filio,
|  \b cum Sancto Spiritu,
|  \b in saecula saeculorum. Amen.
|
|  To you praise is due, to you song is due,
|  to you be glory God the Father and Son,
|  with the Holy Spirit,
|  forever and ever. Amen.
</Poetry>
