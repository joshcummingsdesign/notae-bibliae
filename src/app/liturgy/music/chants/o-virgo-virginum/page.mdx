## History

An eighth [O Antiphon](/glossary/liturgical-terms#o-antiphons) from the medieval English <Definition lang="latin" anchor="usus" text="use" />. The focus is the [Blessed Virgin Mary](/people/saints/biblical/blessed-virgin-mary) (Isa. 7:14). The ancient English practice moves the other O Antiphons back one day, thus beginning on December 16, and ending with this antiphon on December 23.

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b O Virgo virginum, quomodo fiet istud?
|  \b quia nec primam similem visa es, nec habere sequentem.
|  \b Filiae Jerusalem, quid me admiramini?
|  \b Divinum est mysterium, hoc quod cernitis.
|
|  **Literal**
|  O Virgin of virgins, how shall this be?
|  For neither before thee was any like thee, nor shall there be after.
|  Daughters of Jerusalem, why marvel ye at me?
|  The thing which ye behold is a divine mystery.
</Poetry>
