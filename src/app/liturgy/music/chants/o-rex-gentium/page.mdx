## History

The sixth of the [O Antiphons](/glossary/liturgical-terms#o-antiphons) for December 22nd, which dates back to the 6th century. The focus is the Lord as the King of the nations (Isa. 2:4), or in Latin *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="rex" text="Rex" /> <Definition lang="latin" anchor="gentes" text="gentium" />*.

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b O Rex gentium, et desideratus earum,
|  \b lapisque angularis, qui facis utraque unum:
|  \b veni, et salva hominem,
|  \b quem de limo formasti.
|
|  O King of the gentiles, and their desire,
|  the cornerstone, that makest of two one,
|  come and save man,
|  whom you have formed from the dust of the earth.
</Poetry>
