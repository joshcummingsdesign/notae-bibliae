## History

The sixth of the [O Antiphons](/glossary/liturgical-terms#o-antiphons) for December 22nd, which dates back to the 6th century. The focus is the Lord as the King of the nations (Isa. 2:4), or in Latin *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="rex" text="Rex" /> <Definition lang="latin" anchor="gentes" text="gentium" />*. In 1961, French Benedictine monks at Solesmes Abbey (northwest France) published a melody for this chant in the *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="liber-usualis" text="Liber Usualis" />*.

## Text

***O Rex gentium, et desideratus earum,***<br/>
O King of the nations, and their desire,<br/>
***lapisque angularis qui facis utraque unum:***<br/>
the cornerstone making both one:<br/>
***veni et salva hominem,***<br/>
come and save the human race,<br/>
***quem de limo formasti.***<br/>
which you fashioned from clay.
