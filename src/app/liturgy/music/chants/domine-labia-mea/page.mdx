## History

The Domine labia mea (Psalm 51:15) and the Deus in adjutorium (Psalm 70:1) form the opening [versicles](/glossary/liturgical-terms#versicle) for [Matins](/glossary/liturgical-terms#matins).

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b Domine, labia mea aperies.
|  \b Et os meum annuntiabit laudem tuam.
|
|  # O Lord, open thou my lips.[^3]
|  And my mouth shall show forth thy praise.
|
|  \b Deus in adjutorium meum intende.
|  \b Domine ad adjuvandum me festina.
|
|  # O God, make speed to save me.[^4]
|  O Lord, make haste to help me.
|
|  \b Gloria Patri et Filio, et Spiritui Sancto.
|  \b Sicut erat in principio, et nunc, et semper,
|  \b et in saecula saeculorum. Amen.
|
|  \B Glory be to the Father, and to the Son,
|  and to the Holy Ghost.
|  \U As it was in the beginning,
|  is now, and ever shall be,
|  world without end. Amen.
|
|  \b % Alleluia.[^5]
|
|  Praise ye the Lord.
|  The Lord's name be prais-ed.
</Poetry>
