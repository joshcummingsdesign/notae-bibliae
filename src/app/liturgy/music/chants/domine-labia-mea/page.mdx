## History

The [Deus in adjutorium](/liturgy/music/chants/deus-in-adjutorium) (Psalm 70:1) and the Domine labia mea (Psalm 51:15) form the opening [versicles](/glossary/liturgical-terms#versicle) for [Matins](/glossary/liturgical-terms#matins). It was prescribed as such as early as the 6th century in the  [*Rule of Saint Benedict*](/people/saints/medieval/benedict-of-nursia).

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b \V Domine, labia mea aperies.
|  \b \R Et os meum annuntiabit laudem tuam.
|
|  \V # O Lord, open thou my lips.[^3]
|  \R And my mouth shall show forth thy praise.
