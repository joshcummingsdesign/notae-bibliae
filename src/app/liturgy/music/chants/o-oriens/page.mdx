## History

The fifth of the [O Antiphons](/glossary/liturgical-terms#o-antiphons) for December 21st, which dates back to the 6th century. The focus is the Lord as our Morning Star (Isa. 60:1-3), or in Latin *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="oriens" text="Oriens" />*. In 1961, French Benedictine monks at Solesmes Abbey (northwest France) published a melody for this chant in the *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="liber-usualis" text="Liber Usualis" />*.

## Text

***O Oriens,***<br/>
O Morning Star,<br/>
***splendor lucis aeternae, et sol justitiae:***<br/>
splendour of light eternal and sun of righteousness:<br/>
***veni et illumina sedentes in tenebris et umbra mortis.***<br/>
come and enlighten those who dwell in darkness and the shadow of death.
