## History

The fifth of the [O Antiphons](/glossary/liturgical-terms#o-antiphons) for December 21st, which dates back to the 6th century. The focus is the Lord as our Dayspring (Isa. 60:1-3), or in Latin *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="oriens" text="Oriens" />*.

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b O Oriens, splendor lucis aeternae,
|  \b et sol justitiae:
|  \b veni, et illumina
|  \b sedentes in tenebris et umbra mortis.
|
|  O Dayspring, splendor of light eternal,
|  and sun of righteousness:
|  come, and enlighten
|  those who dwell in darkness and the shadow of death.
</Poetry>
