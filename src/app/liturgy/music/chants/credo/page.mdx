## History

The <Definition lang="latin" anchor="credo" text="Credo" /> is the portion of the Mass where the [Nicene Creed](/liturgy/creeds/nicene-creed-with-filioque) is recited or sung. Along with the [Kryie](/liturgy/music/chants/kyrie), [Gloria](/liturgy/music/chants/gloria), [Sanctus](/liturgy/music/chants/sanctus), and [Agnus Dei](/liturgy/music/chants/agnus-dei), it is part of the Ordinary of the Mass.

The setting below is from the *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="liber-usualis" text="Liber Usualis" />* (1961).

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b Credo in unum Deum,
|  I believe in one God,
|  \b Patrem omnipotentem,
|  the Father Almighty,
|  \b Factorem caeli et terrae,
|  Maker of heaven and earth,
|  \b visibilium omnium et invisibilium;
|  of all things visible and invisible;
|
|  \b Et in unum Dominum Iesum Christum,
|  And in one Lord Jesus Christ,
|  \b Filium Dei unigenitum,
|  the only Son of God,
|  \b et ex Patre natum, ante omnia saecula,
|  and begotten of the Father, before all ages,
|  \b Deum de Deo, Lumen de Lumine,
|  God from God, Light from Light,
|  \b Deum verum de Deo vero,
|  true God from true God,
|  \b genitum, non factum, consubstantialem Patri;
|  begotten, not made, consubstantial with the Father;
|  \b Per quem omnia facta sunt.
|  Through him all things were made.
|  \b Qui propter nos homines et propter nostram salutem descendit de caelis,
|  For us men and for our salvation he came down from heaven,
|  \b et incarnatus est de Spiritu Sancto
|  and by the Holy Spirit was incarnate
|  \b ex Maria Virgine, et homo factus est.
|  of the Virgin Mary, and became man.
|  \b Crucifixus etiam pro nobis sub Pontio Pilato,
|  For our sake he was crucified under Pontius Pilate,
|  \b passus et sepultus est,
|  suffered [death] and was buried,
|  \b et resurrexit tertia die, secundum Scripturas,
|  and rose again on the third day, in accordance with the Scriptures,
|  \b et ascendit in caelum; sedet ad dexteram Patris.
|  and ascended into heaven; he sits at the right hand of the Father.
|  \b Et iterum venturus est cum gloria
|  He will come again in glory
|  \b iudicare vivos et mortuos,
|  to judge the living and the dead,
|  \b cuius regni non erit finis.
|  and his kingdom will have no end.
|
|  \b Et in Spiritum Sanctum, Dominum et vivificantem,
|  I believe in the Holy Spirit, the Lord and the Giver of life,
|  \b qui ex Patre Filioque procedit,
|  who proceeds from the Father and the Son,
|  \b qui cum Patre et Filio simul adoratur et conglorificatur,
|  who with the Father and the Son is adored and glorified,
|  \b qui locutus est per prophetas.
|  who has spoken through the prophets.
|
|  \b Et unam, sanctam, catholicam et apostolicam ecclesiam;
|  I believe in one holy, catholic and apostolic Church;
|  \b confiteor unum baptisma in remissionem peccatorum;
|  I confess one Baptism for the remission of sins;
|  \b et exspecto resurrectionem mortuorum,
|  and I look forward to the resurrection of the dead,
|  \b et vitam venturi saeculi. Amen.
|  and the life of the world to come. Amen.
</Poetry>
