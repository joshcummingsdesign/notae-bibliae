## History

The <Definition lang="latin" anchor="credo" text="Credo" /> is the portion of the Mass where the [Nicene Creed](/liturgy/creeds/nicene-creed-with-filioque) is chanted. Along with the [Kryie](/liturgy/music/chants/kyrie), [Gloria](/liturgy/music/chants/gloria), [Sanctus](/liturgy/music/chants/sanctus), and [Agnus Dei](/liturgy/music/chants/agnus-dei), it is part of the Ordinary of the Mass.

The setting below is from the *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="liber-usualis" text="Liber Usualis" />* (1961).

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b Credo in unum Deum,
|  \b Patrem omnipotentem,
|  \b factorem caeli et terrae,
|  \b visibilium omnium,
|  \b et invisibilium.
|
|  I believe in one God,
|  the Father almighty,
|  maker of heaven and earth,
|  of all things visible
|  and invisible.
|
|  \b Et in unum Dominum Jesum Christum,
|  \b Filium Dei unigenitum.
|  \b Et ex Patre natum
|  \b ante omnia saecula.
|
|  And in one Lord Jesus Christ,
|  the only-begotten Son of God.
|  And born of the Father
|  before all ages.
|
|  \b Deum de Deo, lumen de lumine,
|  \b Deum verum de Deo vero.
|  \b Genitum, non factum,
|  \b consubstantialem Patri:
|  \b Per quem omnia facta sunt.
|
|  God from God, light from light,
|  true God from true God.
|  Begotten, not made,
|  consubstantial with the Father:
|  Through him all things were made.
|
|  \b Qui propter nos homines,
|  \b et propter nostram salutem
|  \b descendit de caelis.
|
|  Who for us men,
|  and for our salvation
|  came down from heaven.
|
|  \b Et incarnatus est
|  \b de Spiritu Sancto
|  \b ex Maria Virgine:
|  \b Et homo factus est.
|
|  And was incarnate
|  by the Holy Spirit
|  of the Virgin Mary:
|  and became man.
|
|  \b Crucifixus etiam pro nobis:
|  \b sub Pontio Pilato
|  \b passus, et sepultus est.
|
|  Crucified even for our sake:
|  under Pontius Pilate
|  he suffered, and was buried.
|
|  \b Et resurrexit tertia die,
|  \b secundum Scripturas.
|  \b Et ascendit in caelum:
|  \b sedet ad dexteram Patris.
|
|  And rose again on the third day,
|  in accordance with the Scriptures.
|  And ascended into heaven:
|  and is seated at the right hand of the Father.
|
|  \b Et iterum venturus est cum gloria,
|  \b judicare vivos et mortuos:
|  \b cujus regni non erit finis.
|
|  And he will come again in glory,
|  to judge the living and the dead:
|  whose kingdom will have no end.
|
|  \b Et in Spiritum Sanctum, Dominum
|  \b et vivificantem:
|  \b qui ex Patre Filioque procedit.
|
|  And in the Holy Spirit, the Lord
|  the giver of life:
|  who proceeds from the Father and the Son.
|
|  \b Qui cum Patre et Filio
|  \b simul adoratur,
|  \b et conglorificatur:
|  \b qui locutus est per Prophetas.
|
|  who with the Father and the Son
|  is adored,
|  and glorified:
|  who has spoken through the Prophets.
|
|  \b Et unam sanctam catholicam
|  \b et apostolicam Ecclesiam.
|  \b Confiteor unum baptisma
|  \b in remissionem peccatorum.
|
|  I believe in one holy catholic
|  and apostolic Church.
|  I confess one baptism
|  for the remission of sins.
|
|  \b Et exspecto
|  \b resurrectionem mortuorum.
|  \b Et vitam venturi saeculi. Amen.
|
|  And I look forward
|  to the resurrection of the dead.
|  And the life of the world to come. Amen.
</Poetry>
