## History

*Veni Sancte Spiritus* (Latin: "Come, Holy Spirit"), sometimes called the Golden Sequence, is a <Definition lang="latin" anchor="sequentia" text="sequence" /> used in the Roman Liturgy for the Mass of Pentecost. It dates back to the 12th century, and is usually attributed to Pope Innocent III or to Archbishop of Canterbury Cardinal Stephen Langton, although it has been attributed to others as well. It was published in the *Roman Missal* in 1570.

## Text

**Veni, Sancte Spiritus,**<br/>
Come, Holy Spirit,<br/>
**et emitte caelitus**<br/>
and send out from heaven<br/>
**lucis tuae radium.**<br/>
the ray of your light.

**Veni, pater pauperum,**<br/>
Come, father of the poor,<br/>
**veni, dator munerum,**<br/>
come, giver of gifts,<br/>
**veni, lumen cordium.**<br/>
come, light of hearts.

**Consolator optime,**<br/>
Greatest comforter,<br/>
**dulcis hospes animae,**<br/>
sweet guest of the soul,<br/>
**dulce refrigerium.**<br/>
sweet consolation.

**In labore requies,**<br/>
In labour, rest,<br/>
**in aestu temperies,**<br/>
in heat, temperateness,<br/>
**in fletu solatium.**<br/>
in tears, solace.

**O lux beatissima,**<br/>
O most blessed light,<br/>
**reple cordis intima**<br/>
fill the inmost heart<br/>
**tuorum fidelium.**<br/>
of your faithful.

**Sine tuo numine,**<br/>
Without the nod of your head,<br/>
**nihil est in homine,**<br/>
there is nothing in man,<br/>
**nihil est innoxium.**<br/>
nothing that is harmless.

**Lava quod est sordidum,**<br/>
Cleanse what is unclean,<br/>
**riga quod est aridum,**<br/>
water what is parched,<br/>
**sana quod est saucium.**<br/>
heal what is wounded.

**Flecte quod est rigidum,**<br/>
Bend what is inflexible,<br/>
**fove quod est frigidum,**<br/>
warm what is chilled,<br/>
**rege quod est devium.**<br/>
correct what has gone astray.

**Da tuis fidelibus,**<br/>
Give to your faithful,<br/>
**in te confidentibus,**<br/>
who trust in you,<br/>
**sacrum septenarium.**<br/>
the sevenfold gift.

**Da virtutis meritum,**<br/>
Give virtue's reward,<br/>
**da salutis exitum,**<br/>
give salvation's end,<br/>
**da perenne gaudium.**<br/>
give joy eternal.

**Amen. Alleluia.**
