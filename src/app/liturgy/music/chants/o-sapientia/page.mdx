## History

The first of the [O Antiphons](/glossary/liturgical-terms#o-antiphons) for December 17th, which dates back to the 6th century. The focus is the Lord as Wisdom (Isa. 11:2) or in Latin *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="sapientia" text="Sapientia" />*. In 1961, French Benedictine monks at Solesmes Abbey (northwest France) published a melody for this chant in the *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="liber-usualis" text="Liber Usualis" />*.

## Text

***O Sapientia,***<br/>
O Wisdom,<br/>
***quae ex ore Altissimi prodiisti,***<br/>
coming forth from the mouth of the Most High,<br/>
***attingens a fine usque ad finem,***<br/>
reaching from one end to the other,<br/>
***fortiter suaviterque disponens omnia:***<br/>
mightily and sweetly ordering all things:<br/>
***veni ad docendum nos viam prudentiae.***<br/>
come and teach us the way of prudence.
