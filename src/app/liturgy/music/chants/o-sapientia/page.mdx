## History

The first of the [O Antiphons](/glossary/liturgical-terms#o-antiphons) for December 17th, which dates back to the 6th century. The focus is the Lord as Wisdom (Isa. 11:2) or in Latin *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="sapientia" text="Sapientia" />*.

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b O Sapientia, quae ex ore Altissimi prodiisti,
|  \b attingens a fine usque ad finem,
|  \b fortiter suaviter disponensque omnia:
|  \b veni ad docendum nos viam prudentiae.
|
|  **Literal**
|  O Wisdom, coming forth from the mouth of the Most High,
|  reaching from one end to the other,
|  mightily and sweetly ordering all things:
|  come and teach us the way of prudence.
|
|  **Paraphrase**
|  O come, thou Wis­dom from on high,
|  Who or­der­est all things migh­ti­ly;
|  To us the path of know­ledge show,
|  And teach us in her ways to go.
|  Rejoice! Rejoice! Emmanuel
|  Shall come to thee, O Israel.
</Poetry>

[^1]: "O Sapientia", *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="liber-usualis" text="Liber Usualis" />* (1961), 340
