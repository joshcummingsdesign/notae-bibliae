## History

The Deus in adjutorium (Psalm 70:1) and the [Domine labia mea](/liturgy/music/chants/domine-labia-mea) (Psalm 51:15) form the opening [versicles](/glossary/liturgical-terms#versicle) for [Matins](/glossary/liturgical-terms#matins). The Deus in adjutorium is also used to open [Prime](/glossary/liturgical-terms#prime), [Terce](/glossary/liturgical-terms#terce), [Sext](/glossary/liturgical-terms#sext), and [None](/glossary/liturgical-terms#none). It was prescribed as such as early as the 6th century in the  [*Rule of Saint Benedict*](/people/saints/medieval/benedict-of-nursia).

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b Deus in adjutorium meum intende.
|  \b Domine ad adjuvandum me festina.
|
|  # O God, make speed to save me.[^3]
|  O Lord, make haste to help me.
