## History

An ancient hymn, whose origins can be traced as far back as 252 AD, when [St. Cyprian](/people/saints/latin-fathers/cyprian-of-carthage) drew a striking parallel to its words in his treatise *On Mortality*. Some ancient manuscripts attribute the hymn to [St. Ambrose](/people/saints/latin-fathers/ambrose-of-milan) and [St. Augustine](/people/saints/latin-fathers/augustine-of-hippo), giving rise to the belief that it was composed for Augustine's baptism in 386 AD. More likely, however, their names became associated with the hymn because they helped introduce it into the Western Church. Hence it is sometimes called the "Ambrosian Hymn." Scholars generally agree that, in its present form, the hymn was composed around the 4th century.[^1] By the 6th century, it was prescribed for use at [Matins](/glossary/liturgical-terms#matins) on the Lord's Day in the [*Rule of Saint Benedict*](/people/saints/medieval/benedict-of-nursia).[^2]

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b Te Deum laudamus:
|  \b te Dominum confitemur.
|
|  We praise thee O God;
|  we acknowledge thee to be the Lord.
|
|  \b Te aeternum Patrem
|  \b omnis terra veneratur.
|
|  All the earth doth worship thee
|  the Father everlasting.
|
|  \b Tibi omnes Angeli,
|  \b tibi Caeli et universae Potestates:
|
|  To thee all angels cry aloud;
|  the heavens and all the powers therein;
|
|  \b Tibi Cherubim et Seraphim
|  \b incessabili voce proclamant:
|
|  To thee Cherubim and Seraphim
|  continually do cry,
|
|  \b Sanctus, Sanctus, Sanctus,
|  \b Dominus Deus Sabaoth.
|
|  Holy, Holy, Holy,
|  Lord God of Sabaoth;
|
|  \b Pleni sunt caeli et terra
|  \b majestatis gloriae tuae.
|
|  Heaven and earth are full
|  of the majesty of thy glory.
|
|  \b Te gloriosus
|  \b Apostolorum chorus:
|
|  The glorious company
|  of the apostles praise thee.
|
|  \b Te Prophetarum
|  \b laudabilis numerus:
|
|  The goodly fellowship
|  of the prophets praise thee.
|
|  \b Te Martyrum candidatus
|  \b laudat exercitus.
|
|  The noble army
|  of Martyrs praise thee.
|
|  \b Te per orbem terrarum
|  \b sancta confitetur Ecclesia:
|
|  The holy Church throughout the world
|  doth acknowledge thee;
|
|  \b Patrem
|  \b immensae majestatis:
|
|  The Father
|  of an infinite majesty;
|
|  \b Venerandum tuum verum,
|  \b et unicum Filium:
|
|  Thine adorable, true,
|  and only Son;
|
|  \b Sanctum quoque
|  \b Paraclitum Spiritum.
|
|  Also the Holy Ghost
|  the Comforter.
|
|  \b Tu Rex gloriae, Christe.
|
|  Thou art the King of Glory O Christ.
|
|  \b Tu Patris sempiternus es Filius.
|
|  Thou art the everlasting Son of the Father.
|
|  \b Tu ad liberandum suscepturus hominem,
|  \b non horruisti Virginis uterum.
|
|  When thou tookest upon thee to deliver man,
|  thou didst humble thyself to be born of a Virgin.
|
|  \b Tu devicto mortis aculeo,
|  \b aperuisti credentibus regna caelorum.
|
|  When thou hadst overcome the sharpness of death,
|  thou didst open the kingdom of heaven to all believers.
|
|  \b Tu ad dexteram Dei sedes,
|  \b in gloria Patris.
|
|  Thou sittest at the right hand of God,
|  in the glory of the Father.
|
|  \b Judex crederis
|  \b esse venturus.
|
|  We believe that thou shalt come to be our Judge.
|
|  \b Te ergo quaesumus, tuis famulis subveni,
|  \b quos pretioso sanguine redemisti.
|
|  We therefore pray thee help thy servants
|  whom thou hast redeemed with thy precious blood.
|
|  \b Aeterna fac
|  \b cum Sanctis tuis in gloria numerari.
|
|  Make them to be numbered
|  with thy saints in glory everlasting.
|
|  \b Salvum fac populum tuum Domine,
|  \b et benedic hereditati tuae.
|
|  O Lord save thy people
|  and bless thine heritage.
|
|  \b Et rege eos,
|  \b et extolle illos usque in aeternum.
|
|  Govern them
|  and lift them up forever.
|
|  \b Per singulos dies,
|  \b benedicimus te.
|
|  Day by day
|  we magnify thee;
|
|  \b Et laudamus nomen tuum in saeculum,
|  \b et in saeculum saeculi.
|
|  And we worship thy Name ever
|  world without end.
|
|  \b Dignare Domine die isto
|  \b sine peccato nos custodire.
|
|  Vouchsafe O Lord
|  to keep us this day without sin.
|
|  \b Miserere nostri Domine,
|  \b miserere nostri.
|
|  O Lord have mercy upon us,
|  have mercy upon us.
|
|  \b Fiat misericordia tua Domine super nos,
|  \b quemadmodum speravimus in te.
|
|  O Lord let thy mercy be upon us
|  as our tust is in thee.
|
|  \b In te Domine speravi:
|  \b non confundar in aeternum.
|
|  O Lord in thee have I trusted,
|  let me never be confounded.
</Poetry>
