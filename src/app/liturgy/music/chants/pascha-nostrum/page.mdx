## History

The *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="pascha-nostrum" text="Pascha nostrum" />* refers to a collection of <Definition lang="greek" anchor="antiphona" text="anthems" /> that replace the [*Venite*](/liturgy/music/chants/venite) on Easter Day in the Anglican tradition. The Easter processional before Matins in the [Use of Sarum](/liturgy/rites/sarum-rite) includes an anthem with Paul’s words from Romans: "Christ rising again from the dead, now dieth not ..." (Rom. 6:9-10). The 1549 *Book of Common Prayer* retained this anthem and added "And so likewise count yourselves dead unto sin ..." (Rom 6:11) and "Christ is risen again ..." (1 Cor. 15:20-22). The 1552 Prayer Book added a rubric directing that these anthems replace the *Venite* on Easter Day.[^1] The 1662 Prayer Book added "Christ our Passover ..." (1 Cor. 5:7-8), from which the the whole collection takes its name. The 1662 Prayer Book also added the [*Gloria Patri*](/liturgy/music/chants/gloria-patri) at the end.[^2] The *Pascha nostrum* is also sometimes referred to as the "Easter Anthems".

## Text

<Poetry>
|  **ALLELUIA.** Christ our Passover is sacrific-ed for us:
|  therefore let us keep the feast,
|
|  Not with old leaven, neither with the leaven of malice and wickedness;
|  but with the unleaven-ed bread of sincerity and truth, alleluia.[^4]
|
|  **CHRIST** being rais-ed from the dead dieth no more;
|  death hath no more dominion over him.
|
|  For in that he di-ed he di-ed unto sin once:
|  but in that he liveth he liveth unto God.
|
|  Likewise reckon ye also yourselves to be dead indeed unto sin,
|  but alive unto God through Jesus Christ our Lord, alleluia.[^5]
|
|  **CHRIST** is risen from the dead,
|  and become the firstfruits of them that slept.
|
|  For since by man came death,
|  by man came also the resurrection of the dead.
|
|  For as in Adam all die,
|  even so in Christ shall all be made alive.[^6]
|
|  Glory be to the Father and to the Son
|  and to the Holy Ghost;
|
|  As it was in the beginning,
|  is now and ever shall be
|  world without end. Amen, alleluia.
</Poetry>
