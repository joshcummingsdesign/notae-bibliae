## History

The third of the [O Antiphons](/glossary/liturgical-terms#o-antiphons) for December 19th, which dates back to the 6th century. The focus is the Lord as the Root of Jesse (Isa. 11:10), or in Latin *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="radix" text="Radix Jesse" />*. In 1961, French Benedictine monks at Solesmes Abbey (northwest France) published a melody for this chant in the *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="liber-usualis" text="Liber Usualis" />*.

## Text

***O radix Jesse,***<br/>
O Root of Jesse,<br/>
***qui stas in signum populorum,***<br/>
standing as a sign among the peoples;<br/>
***super quem continebunt reges os suum,***<br/>
before you kings will shut their mouths,<br/>
***quem gentes deprecabuntur:***<br/>
to you the nations will make their prayer:<br/>
***veni ad liberandum nos, jam noli tardare.***<br/>
come and deliver us, and delay no longer.
