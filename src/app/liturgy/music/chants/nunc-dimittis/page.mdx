## History

The <Definition lang="latin" anchor="nunc-dimittis" text="Nunc dimittis" /> is a Gospel [canticle](/glossary/liturgical-terms#canticle) for [Compline](/glossary/liturgical-terms#compline). The words come from Luke 2:29–32, Simeon's blessing to the baby Jesus when he was presented at the temple in Jerusalem. The [Magnificat](/liturgy/music/chants/magnificat), [Benedictus](/liturgy/music/chants/benedictus), and Nunc dimittis form the first three canticles in the Gospel of Luke.

## Text

**Nunc dimittis servum tuum, Domine,**<br/>
**secundum verbum tuum in pace:**<br/>
Now you are letting your servant depart in peace, O Lord,<br/>
according to your word in peace;

**quia viderunt oculi mei**<br/>
**salutare tuum**<br/>
for my eyes have seen<br/>
your salvation

**quod parasti**<br/>
**ante faciem omnium populorum,**<br/>
that you have prepared<br/>
in the presence of all peoples,

**lumen ad revelationem gentium,**<br/>
**et gloriam plebis tuae Israel.**<br/>
a light for revelation to the Gentiles,<br/>
and for glory to your people Israel.
