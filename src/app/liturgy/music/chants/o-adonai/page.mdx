## History

The second of the [O Antiphons](/glossary/liturgical-terms#o-antiphons) for December 18th, which dates back to the 6th century. The focus is God as Lord (Isa. 33:22), or in Hebrew *<Definition lang="hebrew" anchor="adonai" text="Adonai" />*. In 1961, French Benedictine monks at Solesmes Abbey (northwest France) published a melody for this chant in the *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="liber-usualis" text="Liber Usualis" />*.

## Text

***O Adonai, et Dux domus Israel,***<br/>
O Adonai, and leader of the House of Israel,<br/>
***qui Moysi in igne flammae rubi apparuisti,***<br/>
who appeared to Moses in the fire of the burning bush,<br/>
***et ei in Sina legem dedisti:***<br/>
and gave him the law on Sinai:<br/>
***veni ad redimendum nos in brachio extento.***<br/>
come and redeem us with an outstretched arm.
