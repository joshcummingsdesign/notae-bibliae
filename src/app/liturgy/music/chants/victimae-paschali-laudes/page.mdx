## History

*Victimae Paschali Laudes* (Latin: <Definition lang="latin" anchor="paschalis" text='"Praises to the Paschal Victim"' />) is a <Definition lang="latin" anchor="sequentia" text="sequence" /> for Easter. It is usually attributed to the 11th-century Wipo of Burgundy (chaplain to Holy Roman Emperor Conrad II), but has also been attributed to Notker Balbulus (Swiss Benedictine monk) and others. It was published in the *Roman Missal* in 1570.

## Text

***Victimae paschali laudes***<br/>
***immolent Christiani.***<br/>
Let Christians offer sacrificial<br/>
praises to the passover victim.

***Agnus redemit oves:***<br/>
***Christus innocens Patri***<br/>
***reconciliavit peccatores.***<br/>
The lamb has redeemed the sheep:<br/>
The Innocent Christ has reconciled<br/>
the sinners to the Father.

***Mors et vita duello***<br/>
***conflixere mirando:***<br/>
***dux vitae mortuus,***<br/>
***regnat vivus.***<br/>
Death and life contended<br/>
in a spectacular battle:<br/>
the dead leader of life<br/>
reigns alive.

***Dic nobis Maria,***<br/>
***quid vidisti in via?***<br/>
Tell us, Mary, what did<br/>
you see on the way?

***Sepulcrum Christi viventis,***<br/>
***et gloriam vidi resurgentis***<br/>
"I saw the tomb of the living Christ<br/>
and the glory of his rising,

***Angelicos testes,***<br/>
***sudarium, et vestes.***<br/>
The angelic witnesses, the<br/>
shroud, and the clothes."

***Surrexit Christus spes mea:***<br/>
***praecedet suos in Galilaeam.***<br/>
"Christ my hope is arisen;<br/>
he will go before his own into Galilee."

***Scimus Christum surrexisse***<br/>
***a mortuis vere:***<br/>
***tu nobis, victor Rex, miserere.***<br/>
***Amen. Alleluia.***<br/>
We know Christ is truly risen from the dead!<br/>
On us, you conqueror, King, have mercy!<br/>
Amen. Alleluia.
