## History

A [canticle](/glossary/liturgical-terms#canticle) for [Lauds](/glossary/liturgical-terms#lauds). The words come from the *Prayer of Azariah and Song of the Three Holy Children*, which is found in the Septuagint version of Daniel 3 and was included in the Vulgate. It echoes the praise of Psalm 148.

[^2]: "The Benedicite", *Saint Dunstan's Psalter*, 299–300, par. 3, The last two verses, which form the proper [doxology](/glossary/liturgical-terms#doxology) and were found in the original Latin text, were replaced with the [*Gloria Patri*](/liturgy/music/chants/gloria-patri) in the 1549 *Book of Common Prayer*.
