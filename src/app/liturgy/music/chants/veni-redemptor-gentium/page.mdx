## History

A Latin Advent hymn by [St. Ambrose](/people/saints/latin-fathers/ambrose-of-milan) written in the 4th century. The hymn is sung at [Matins](/glossary/liturgical-terms#matins) in the Liturgy of the Hours during the last week of Advent.

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b Veni, redemptor gentium,
|  \b ostende partum Virginis;
|  \b miretur omne saeculum:
|  \b talis decet partus Deum.
|
|  Come, thou Redeemer of the earth,
|  and manifest thy virgin-birth:
|  let every age adoring fall;
|  such birth befits the God of all.
|
|  \b Non ex virili semine,
|  \b sed mystico spiramine
|  \b Verbum Dei factum est caro
|  \b fructusque ventris floruit.
|
|  Begotten of no human will,
|  But of the Spirit, thou art still
|  The Word of God in flesh arrayed,
|  The promised fruit to men displayed.
|
|  \b Alvus tumescit Virginis,
|  \b claustrum pudoris permanent,
|  \b vexilla virtutum micant,
|  \b versatur in templo Deus.
|
|  The virgin womb that burden gained
|  With virgin honor all unstained;
|  The banners there of virtue glow;
|  God in his temple dwells below.
|
|  \b Procedat e thalamo suo,
|  \b pudoris aula regia,
|  \b geminae gigas substantiae,
|  \b alacris ut currat viam.
|
|  Forth from his chamber goeth he,
|  That royal home of purity,
|  A giant in two-fold substance one,
|  Rejoicing now his course to run.
|
|  \b Aequalis aeterno Patri,
|  \b carnis tropaeo cingere,
|  \b infirma nostri corporis
|  \b virtute firmans perpeti.
|
|  O equal to the Father, thou!
|  Gird on thy fleshly mantle now;
|  The weakness of our mortal state
|  With deathless might invigorate.
|
|  \b Praesepe iam fulget tuum
|  \b lumenque nox spirat novum,
|  \b quod nulla nox interpolet
|  \b fideque iugi luceat.
|
|  Thy cradle here shall glitter bright
|  And darkness breathe a newer light,
|  Where endless faith shall shine serene,
|  And twilight never intervene.
|
| \b Sit, Christe, rex piissime,
| \b tibi Patrique gloria
| \b cum Spiritu Paraclito,
| \b in sempiterna saecula. Amen.
|
|  All praise, eternal Son, to Thee,
|  whose advent sets thy people free,
|  whom, with the Father, we adore,
|  and Holy Ghost, forevermore. Amen.
</Poetry>
