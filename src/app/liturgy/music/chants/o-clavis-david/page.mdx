## History

The fourth of the [O Antiphons](/glossary/liturgical-terms#o-antiphons) for December 20th, which dates back to the 6th century. The focus is the Lord as the Key of David (Isa. 22:22), or in Latin *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="clavis" text="Clavis David" />*.

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b O Clavis David, et sceptrum domus Israel:
|  \b qui aperis, et nemo claudit;
|  \b claudis, et nemo aperit:
|  \b veni et educ vinctum de domo carceris,
|  \b sedentem in tenebris et umbra mortis.
|
|  O Key of David, and scepter of the house of Israel:
|  you open, and no one can shut;
|  you shut, and no one can open:
|  come and lead the prisoners from the prison house,
|  those who dwell in darkness and the shadow of death.
</Poetry>

[^1]: "O Clavis David", *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="liber-usualis" text="Liber Usualis" />* (1961), 341
