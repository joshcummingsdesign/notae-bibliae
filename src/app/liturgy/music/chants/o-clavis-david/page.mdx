## History

The fourth of the [O Antiphons](/glossary/liturgical-terms#o-antiphons) for December 20th, which dates back to the 6th century. The focus is the Lord as the Key of David (Isa. 22:22), or in Latin *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="clavis" text="Clavis David" />*. In 1961, French Benedictine monks at Solesmes Abbey (northwest France) published a melody for this chant in the *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="liber-usualis" text="Liber Usualis" />*.

## Text

***O Clavis David et sceptrum domus Israel;***<br/>
O Key of David and sceptre of the House of Israel;<br/>
***qui aperis et nemo claudit;***<br/>
you open and no one can shut;<br/>
***claudis et nemo aperit:***<br/>
you shut and no one can open:<br/>
***veni et educ vinctum de domo carceris,***<br/>
come and lead the prisoners from the prison house,<br/>
***sedentem in tenebris et umbra mortis.***<br/>
those who dwell in darkness and the shadow of death.
