## History

The <Definition lang="latin" anchor="agnus-dei" text="Agnus Dei" /> is based on John 1:29, "Behold, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!". In the Latin Mass, the Agnus Dei comes between the Lord's Prayer and the Eucharist, and has been part of the Mass since at least the 7th century.[^1] Along with the [Kyrie](/liturgy/music/chants/kyrie), [Gloria](/liturgy/music/chants/gloria), [Credo](/liturgy/music/chants/credo), and [Sanctus](/liturgy/music/chants/sanctus), it is part of the Ordinary of the Mass.

The settings below are from the *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="liber-usualis" text="Liber Usualis" />* (1961).

## Text

<Poetry>
|  \b Agnus Dei, qui tollis peccata mundi,
|  \b miserere nobis.
|
|  Lamb of God, who takes away the sins of the world,
|  have mercy on us.
|
|  \b Agnus Dei, qui tollis peccata mundi,
|  \b miserere nobis.
|
|  Lamb of God, who takes away the sins of the world,
|  have mercy on us.
|
|  \b Agnus Dei, qui tollis peccata mundi,
|  \b dona nobis pacem.
|
|  Lamb of God, who takes away the sins of the world,
