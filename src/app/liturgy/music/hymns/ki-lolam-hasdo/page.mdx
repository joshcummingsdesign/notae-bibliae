## History

The words to the hymn *<Definition lang="hebrew" anchor="ki-lolam-hasdo" text="Ki L'olam Hasdo" />* come directly from Psalm 118:29. Psalm 118 is the last of the <Definition lang="hebrew" anchor="hallel" text="Egyptian Hallel Psalms" /> traditionally sung at Passover. The melody was composed by American Jewish music director Deborah Sacks Mintz in 2026. The melody was originally written for Psalm 136, but is here set to Psalm 118 in order to demonstrate the chanting of the Hallel Psalms (Matt. 26:30).
