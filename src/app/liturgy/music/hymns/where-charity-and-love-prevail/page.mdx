## History

*"Ubi caritas"* (Latin: Where there is charity) is a hymn of the Western Church, long used as one of the antiphons for the washing of feet on Maundy Thursday. Its text is attributed to [St. Paulinus of Aquileia](/liturgy/music/composers/paulinus-of-aquileia) in 796 AD. In 1960, American Catholic hymnist Omer Westendorf translated the song into English as "Where Charity and Love Prevail" and set it to the tune "Christian Love", an adaptation of the tune [*Veni redemptor gentium*](/liturgy/music/chants/veni-redemptor-gentium) arranged by the French Benedictine monk Paul Benoit.

## Text

<Poetry>
|  Where charity and love prevail,
|  There God is ever found;
|  Brought here together by Christ's love,
|  By love are we thus bound.
|
|  With grateful joy and holy fear
|  His charity we learn;[^1]
|  Let us with heart and mind and soul
|  Now love him in return.[^2]
|
|  Forgive we now each other's faults
|  As we our faults confess;
|  And let us love each other well
|  In Christian holiness.
|
|  Let strife among us be unknown,
|  Let all contention cease;
|  Be his the glory that we seek,[^3]
|  Be ours his holy peace.[^4]
|
|  Let us recall that in our midst
|  Dwells God's begotten son;
|  As members of his body joined,
|  We are in him made one.[^5]
|
|  No race nor creed can love exclude,
|  If honored be God's name;
|  Our family embraces all
|  Whose Father is the same.
</Poetry>

[^1]: Or "God's charity we learn"
[^2]: Or "Now love God in return"
[^3]: Or "Be God's the glory that we seek"
[^4]: Or "Be ours God's holy peace"
[^5]: Or "We are in Christ made one"
