## History

The words to the hymn *<Definition lang="hebrew" anchor="baruch-haba" text='"Baruch Haba"' />* come directly from Psalm 118:26-29—the last of the <Definition lang="hebrew" anchor="hallel" text="Egyptian Hallel Psalms" /> (Psalms 113-118). The melody was composed by the Jewish cantor Carl Urstein in 1978.

## Text

<Poetry>
|   **בָּרוּךְ הַבָּא בְּשֵׁם יְהֹוָה**
|  [Baruch haba beshem adonai]
|  Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord.
|
|   **בֵּרַכְנוּכֶם מִבֵּית יְהֹוָה**
|  [Berachnuchem mibeit adonai]
|  From the house of the Lord we bless you.
|
|   **אֵל יְהֹוָה וַיָּאֶר לָנוּ**
|  [El adonai va'yaer lanu]
|  The Lord is God, and he has made his light shine on us.
|
|   **אִסְרוּ־חַג בַּעֲבֹתִים עַד־קַרְנוֹת הַמִּזְבֵּחַ**
|  [Isru chag be'avotim ad karnot hamizbeiach]
|  With boughs in hand, join in the festal procession up to the horns of the altar.
|
|   **אֵלִי אַתָּה וְאוֹדֶךָ**
|  [Eili atah ve'odecha]
|  You are my God, and I will praise you;
|
|   **אֱלֹהַי אֲרוֹמְמֶךָ**
|  [Elohai aromemekha]
|  You are my God, and I will exalt you.
|
|   **הוֹדוּ לַיהֹוָה כִּי־טוֹב**
|  [Hodu ladonai ki tov]
|  Give thanks to the Lord, for he is good;
|
|   **כִּי לְעוֹלָם חַסְדּוֹ**
|  [Ki leolam hasdo]
|  His love endures forever.
</Poetry>
