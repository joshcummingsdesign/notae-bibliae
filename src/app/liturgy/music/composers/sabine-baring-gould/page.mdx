<Initial text="Sabine Baring-Gould" />

Rev. Sabine Baring-Gould (1834–1924) was an English Anglican priest, [hagiographer](/glossary/liturgical-terms#hagiography), and hymnist. In 1852, he was admitted to Cambridge University, earning the degrees of Bachelor of Arts in 1857, then Master of Arts in 1860 from Clare College, Cambridge. He took holy orders in 1864, and at age 30, became the curate at Horbury Bridge in West Yorkshire. In 1865, he wrote the hymn ["Onward, Christian Soldiers"](/liturgy/music/hymns/onward-christian-soldiers). Baring-Gould became the rector of East Mersea in Essex (southeast England) in 1871 and spent ten years there. A prolific writer, Baring-Gould published between 1872 and 1877 his monumental sixteen-volume hagiographical collection, *Lives of the Saints*—a work remarkable for its scale and breadth.
