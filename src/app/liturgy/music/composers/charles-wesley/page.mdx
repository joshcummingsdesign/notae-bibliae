Charles Wesley (1707–1788) was an 18th century priest in the Church of England. He and his brother, John Wesley, were leaders of the Methodist movement. Wesley was a prolific hymnist who wrote over 6,500 hymns during his lifetime including ["Christ the Lord Is Risen Today"](/liturgy/music/hymns/christ-the-lord-is-risen-today), ["Hark! The Herald Angels Sing"](/liturgy/music/hymns/hark-the-herald-angels-sing), ["O for a Thousand Tongues to Sing"](/liturgy/music/hymns/o-for-a-thousand-tongues-to-sing), and ["Come, Thou Long Expected Jesus"](/liturgy/music/hymns/come-thou-long-expected-jesus).
