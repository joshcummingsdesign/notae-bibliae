<Initial text="Sign of the Cross" />

In the West, the [Sign of the Cross](/meditations/the-history-of-the-sign-of-the-cross) is traditionally made by placing the left hand under the breast, performing a left cross with the fingers extended on the right hand, and joining the hands together at the *Amen*.[^1] Patristic evidence, however, suggests that this form developed from the right cross, more commonly performed in the East.[^2] While both options are suitable for any pious Christian, the earlier form will be outlined below.

## Trinitarian Formula

<Poetry>
|  In the name of the Father,
|      and of the Son,
|          and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.[^3]
</Poetry>

#### Right Hand
- Jesus sits at the right hand of God the Father.[^4]

#### Three Fingers
- Represents the Holy Trinity.[^5]

#### Two Fingers
- Represents the two natures of Christ.[^6]

 ## Motion

#### Forehead
- "In the name of the Father..."
- The Father is the Head.[^7]
- The Father is the Principle without a principle.[^8]

#### Chest
- "And of the Son..."
- The downward motion represents the Incarnation.[^9]

#### Right Shoulder
- "And of the Holy..."
- The right is a biblical symbol of power.[^10]
- The shoulders are also a biblical symbol of power.[^11]

#### Left Shoulder
- "...Spirit."
- The left is a biblical symbol of darkness.[^12]
- The right-to-left motion represents how Christ overcame the devil by the power of the Holy Spirit.[^13][^14]
