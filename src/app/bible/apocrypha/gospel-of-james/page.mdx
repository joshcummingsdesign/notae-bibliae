## Synopsis

The Gospel of James is a 2nd-century apocryphal book written in Greek and circulated under the name of [St. James the Less](/people/saints/apostles/james-the-less). It is a commentary on the birth narratives found in the [Gospel of Matthew](/bible/new-testament/gospels/matthew) and the [Gospel of Luke](/bible/new-testament/gospels/luke). It was deemed apocryphal by [St. Jerome](/people/saints/latin-fathers/jerome) in the 4th century.
