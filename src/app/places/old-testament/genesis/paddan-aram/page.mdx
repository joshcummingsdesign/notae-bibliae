- Paddan-aram (Hebrew: פַּדָּן אֲרָם, "the field of Aram") is named after Aram, the son of Shem (Gen. 10:22). The term "Aramean" is derived from his name (Gen. 25:20).

- Refers to [Haran](/places/old-testament/genesis/haran) in Upper Mesopotamia (modern southeastern Turkey).
