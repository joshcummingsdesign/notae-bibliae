<Initial text="Hebrew" />

<p id="adonai">Adonái – אֲדֹנָי – "the Lord" or "lord"</p>

<p id="amen">Amén – אָמֵן – "truly" or "let it be so"</p>

<p id="asham">Ashám – אָשָׁם – "guilt offering"</p>

<p id="avon">Avón – עָוֹן – "bent", "crooked", "injustice", "corruption", "iniquity", creates a burden that one must bear</p>

<p id="bamidbar">Bamidbár – בַּמִּדְבָּר – "in the wilderness"</p>

<p id="barakh">Barákh – בָּרַךְ – "to kneel", "to bestow favor upon", or "to bless"</p>

<p id="bereshit">Bereshít – בְּרֵאשִׁית – "in the beginning"</p>

<p id="bikkurim">Bikkurím – בִּכּוּרִים – "firstfuits", as in the Feast of Firstfruits</p>

<p id="birkat-hamazon">Birkát Hamazón – בִּרְכַּת הַמָּזוׂן – "the blessing of the food", a blessing or grace after meals</p>

<p id="david">Davíd – דָּוִד – "beloved"</p>

<p id="devarim">Devarím – דְּבָרִים – "words"</p>

<p id="divrei-hayamim">Div'rei hayamím – דִּבְרֵי יָמִים – "Words of the Day"</p>

<p id="ehye">Ehyé – אֶהְיֶה – "I AM" or "I WILL BE", what the Lord calls himself at the burning bush</p>

<p id="el">El – אֵל – "God", "god", or "spiritual being", short form of elóah</p>

<p id="eloha">Elóah – אֱלוֹהַּ – "God", "god", or "spiritual being"</p>

<p id="elohim">Elohím – אֱלֹהִים – "God", "god(s)", or "spiritual being(s)", plural of elóah, but is sometimes used as a singular, almost like saying "the skies" to mean simply "the sky"</p>

<p id="ester">Estér – אֶסְתֵּר – "star", from Persian</p>

<p id="ezra">Ezrá – עֶזְרָא – "help"</p>

<p id="hadasa">Hadasá – הֲדַסָּה – "myrtle", an evergreen shrub</p>

<p id="halal">Halál – הָלַל – "to priase"</p>

<p id="hallel">Hallél – הַלֵּל – "praise", Psalms 113–118 are known as the Egyptian Hallel Psalms, and are sung in connection with the Passover</p>

<p id="hallelu">Hallelú – הַלְּלוּ – "praise ye!"</p>

<p id="halleluya">Halleluyá – הַלְלוּיָהּ – "praise ye the Lord!"</p>

<p id="hanukkah">Hanukkáh – חֲנֻכָּה – "the Feast of Dedication"</p>

<p id="har-megido">Har megído – הַר מְגִדּוֹ – "Mount Megiddo"</p>

<p id="hashem">Hashém – הַשֵּׁם – "The Name", referring to the Tetragrammaton</p>

<p id="havdalah">Havdaláh – הַבְדָּלָה – "separation", as in the blessing at the end of the Sabbath</p>

<p id="herem">Hérem – חֵרֶם – "a thing dedicated or devoted", i.e. that which has completely transitioned from one status to another, such as from unclean to holy, or from holy to cut off and accursed</p>

<p id="hevel">Hével – הֶבֶל – "vanity", "mist", "vapor", "fleeting", or "elusive"</p>

<p id="hoshia-na">Hoshía ná – הוֹשִׁיעָה נָּא – "O save"</p>

<p id="immanuel">Immanuél – עִמָּנוּאֵל – "God with us"</p>

<p id="iyov">Iyóv – אִיּוֹב – "where is the father?", "enemy"</p>

<p id="kaddish">Kaddísh – קַדִּישׁ – "sanctification", as in a hymn praising God that is recited during Jewish prayer services</p>

<p id="kadosh">Kadósh – קָדוֹשׁ – "holy"</p>

<p id="kalal">Kalál – קָלַל – "to lighten" or "to curse"</p>

<p id="karav">Karáv – קָרַב – "to approach" or "to draw near"</p>

<p id="kavod">Kavód – כָּבוֹד – "heaviness", "honor", or "glory"</p>

<p id="kedushah">Kedusháh – קְדֻשָּׁה – "sanctification", as in a set of prayers during the Jewish liturgy which includes Isa. 6:3</p>

<p id="keruvim">Keruvím – כְּרוּבִים – "cherubim", plural of "cherub" (Hebrew: kerúv), heavenly beings, they guard the tree of life, two gold cherubim adorned the ends of the mercy seat, the veil in the tabernacle had a cherubim pattern on it, two gold cherubim stood in the Most Holy Place in the temple, Ezekiel describes them as having four wings and four faces (human, lion, ox, and eagle)</p>

<p id="ketuvim">Ketuvím – כְּתוּבִים – "writings"</p>

<p id="khata">Khatá – חָטָא – "to miss the mark", "to sin"</p>

<p id="khatat">Khatát – חַטָּאָת – "sin offering"</p>

<p id="kiddush">Kiddúsh – קִדּוּשׁ – "sanctification", as in a blessing said over the wine on a Jewish holy day</p>

<p id="kohelet">Kohélet – קֹהֶלֶת – "teacher"</p>

<p id="korban">Korbán – קָרְבָּן – "offering", from karáv</p>

<p id="maggid">Maggíd – מַגִּיד – "narrator", "teacher", or "preacher"</p>

<p id="mashiakh">Mashíakh – מָשִׁיחַ – "Anointed One", "anointed", "Messiah", or "Christ"</p>

<p id="matzah">Matzáh – מַצָּה – "unleavened bread"</p>

<p id="megillot">Megillot – מְגִילוֹת – "scrolls"</p>

<p id="melachim">Melachim – מְלָכִים – "kings"</p>

<p id="midrash">Midrásh – מִדְרָשׁ – A Rabbinic commentary on a text from the Scriptures</p>

<p id="minkha">Minkhá – מִנְחָה – "gift", "tribute", "meal offering", or "grain offering"</p>

<p id="mishkan">Mishkán – מִשְׁכָּן – "the tabernacle"</p>

<p id="mishlei">Mishléi – מִשְׁלֵי – "proverbs"</p>

<p id="nechemya">Nechemyá – נְחֶמְיָה – "consolation of the Lord"</p>

<p id="nephesh">Néphesh – נֶפֶשׁ – "soul", in the Bible, we do not have souls, we are souls</p>

<p id="neviim">Nevi'im – נְבִיאִים – "prophets"</p>

<p id="nshama">N'shamá – נְשָׁמָה – "breath", "breath of life", or "spirit"</p>

<p id="olah">Oláh – עֹלָה – "that which goes up or ascends", "burnt offering"</p>

<p id="omer">Ómer – עֹמֶר – "sheaf", "bundle of grain", or "about 2 quarts"</p>

<p id="pesach">Pésach – פֶּסַח – "the Passover"</p>

<p id="pesha">Pésha – פֶּשַׁע – "rebellion", "violation of trust", "breaking of a covenant", "transgression"</p>

<p id="rosh-hashana">Rosh hashaná – רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה – "head of the year", as in the Feast of Trumpets</p>

<p id="ruakh">Rúakh – רוּחַ – "breath", "breath of life", "wind", "spirit", "spiritual being", or "Holy Spirit"</p>

<p id="ruth">Ruth – רוּת – "companion"</p>

<p id="shaul">Sha'úl – שָׁאוּל – "asked for"</p>

<p id="salal">Salál – סָלַל – "to clear the way", "to lift up", or "to exalt"</p>

<p id="shmuel">Shmu'él – שְׁמוּאֵל – "heard by God"</p>

<p id="satan">Satán – שָׂטָן – "the Adversary" or "the Accuser"</p>

<p id="seder">Séder – סֵדֶר – "order", e.g. a ceremonial meal</p>

<p id="selah">Sélah – סֶלָה – "to clear the way", "to lift up", or "to exalt", from salál</p>

<p id="seraphim">Seraphím – שְׂרָפִים – "seraphim", heavenly beings, plural of "seráph", which means "to burn" or "fiery serpent", Isaiah sees them in the Lord's heavenly throne room and describes them as having six wings</p>

<p id="shabbat">Shabbát – שַׁבָּת – "rest", "the Sabbath"</p>

<p id="shabbat-shalom">Shabbát shalóm – שַׁבָּת שָׁלוֹם – "peaceful Sabbath"</p>

<p id="shalom">Shalóm – שָׁלוֹם – "peace", "rest", "hello", "goodbye"</p>

<p id="shavuot">Shavu'ót – שָׁבוּעוֹת – "weeks", as in the Feast of Weeks, which is also called the Feast of the Harvest or Pentecost</p>

<p id="shelem">Shelém – שֶׁלֶם – "peace offering"</p>

<p id="shemot">Shemót – שְׁמוֹת – "names"</p>

<p id="shlomo">Shlomó – שְׁלֹמֹה – "whole" or "complete"</p>

<p id="shofar">Shofár – שׁוֹפָר – "a ram's-horn trumpet"</p>

<p id="shofetim">Shofetím – שׁוֹפְטִים – "judges"</p>

<p id="sukkot">Sukkót – סֻכּוֹת – "booths", as in the Feast of Booths</p>

<p id="tehillim">Tehillím – תְּהִלִּים – "praises"</p>

<p id="torah">Toráh – תּוֹרָה – "teaching"</p>

<p id="vayikra">Vayikrá – וַיִּקְרָא – "and he called"</p>

<p id="yedidya">Yedidyá – יְהוֹשֻׁעַ – "beloved of the Lord"</p>

<p id="yehoshua">Yehoshúa – יְהוֹשֻׁעַ – "the Lord saves"</p>

<p id="yhwh">YHWH – יהוה – "HE IS" or "HE WILL BE", the Tetragrammaton, from ehyé, what the Lord tells Moses to call him at the burning bush, most scholars believe it is pronounced "Yahweh"</p>

<p id="yom-kippur">Yom Kippúr – יוֹם כִּיפּוּר – "the Day of Atonement"</p>

<p id="yom-teruah">Yom Teruáh – יוֹם תְּרוּעָה – "the day of shouting or blasting", as in the Feast of Trumpets</p>

<p id="yona">Yoná – יוֹנָה – "dove"</p>

<p id="zerubbabel">Zerubbabél – זְרֻבָּבֶל – "planted in Babylon"</p>
