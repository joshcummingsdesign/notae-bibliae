## Ambrose

The Greek name *Ambrósios* means "immortal" or "divine". The Latinized version is *Ambrosius*. In English it is Ambrose.

## Andrew

The Greek name *Andréas* (Ἀνδρέας) means "manly". In French it is *André*. In English it is Andrew.

## Anne

The Hebrew name *Hannah* (חַנָּה) means "grace" or "favor". In Greek it is *Anna* (Ἄννα). The shortened Latinized version is Ann, which is also spelled "Anne".

## Anselm

The name Anselm is of Germanic origin and means "God's helmet".

## Anthony

The Latin name *Antonius* is a family name most likely of Etruscan (modern Italian) origin. It sounds like the Greek *ánthos* (ἄνθος), meaning "flower".

## Aphrahat

The Syriac name *Aphrahat* (ܐܦܪܗܛ) means "gained" or "earned".

## Athanasius

The Greek name Athanásios (Ἀθανάσιος) means "immortal". The Latinized version is Athanasius.

## Augustine

The Latin name *Augustinus* comes from the Latin *Augustus*, meaning "majestic" or "venerable". In English it is Augustine.

## Barnabas

The Aramaic name *Barneḇú'â* (בַרְנֶבֻאָה) means "prophet's son" or "son of encouragement". The Latinized version of his name is Barnabas.

## Bartholomew

The Aramaic name *Bar-Tólmai* (בר-תולמי) means "son of the furrows". In Greek it is *Bartholomaîos* (Βαρθολομαῖος). In English it is Bartholomew.

## Basil

The Greek name *Basíleios* (Βασίλειος) means "royal". The Latinized version is *Basilius*. In English it is Basil.

## Benedict

The name Benedict is derived from the Latin *Benedictus*, meaning "blessed".

## Bernard

The name Bernhard is of Frankish origin and is composed of the two elements bern "bear" and hard "brave, hardy", thus it means "bold as a bear". The Latinized version is *Bernardus*, and the English version is Bernard.

## Boethius

The Greek name *Boēthós* (βοηθός) means "helper". The Latinized version is Boethius.

## Bonaventure

The name Bonaventure comes from the Latin *bonus* which means "good", and *ventūra* which means "the things to come". Thus, it means "good things to come" or "good fortune".

## Christopher

The Greek name *Khrīstóphoros* (Χρῑστόφορος) means "Christ-bearer". The Latinized version is *Chrīstophorus*. In English it is Christopher.

## Chrysostom

The Greek name *Khrusóstomos* (Χρυσόστομος) means "golden-mouthed", and denotes great eloquence.

## Clement

The Latin name *Clēmens* means "merciful". In English it is Clement.

## Cleopas

The Hebrew name *Heleph* (חֵלֶף) means "to change". It can also be pronounced *Cheleph*. With the Greek termination it written as either Alphaeus (Matt. 10:3), Cleopas (Luke 24:18), or Clopas (John 19:25).

## Cyprian

The Latin name *Cyprianus* means "of Cyprus". In English it is Cyprian.

## Cyril

The Greek name *Kúrillos* (Κύριλλος) means "lordly". The Latinized version is *Cȳrillus*. In English it is Cyril.

## Damasus

The Greek name *Dámasos* (Δάμασος) comes from the greek *damázō* (δαμάζω), meaning "to tame", "to subdue", or "to rule over". The Latinized version is Damasus.

## Dominic

The Latin name *Dominicus* means "of the Lord". In English it is Dominic.

## Ephraim

The Hebrew name *Efráyim* (אֶפְרַיִם) means "fruitful". In English it is Ephraim.

## Epiphanius

The Greek name *Epiphánios* (Ἐπιφάνιος) comes from the Greek *epĭphắneiă* (ἐπῐφᾰ́νειᾰ), meaning "appearance" or "manifestation". The Latinized version is Epiphanius.

## Eusebius

The Greek name *Eusébios* (Εὐσέβιος) means "pious". The Latinized version is Eusebius.

## Francis

The Latin name *Franciscus* means "Frenchman". In Italian it is *Francesco*. In English it is Francis.

## Gabriel

The Hebrew name *Gaḇrīʾḗl* (גַּבְרִיאֵל) means "God is my strength". In English it is Gabriel.

## Gregory

The Greek name *Grēgórios* (Γρηγόριος) means "watchful" or "vigilant". The Latinized version is *Grēgorius*. In English it is Gregory.

## Hilary

The Latin name *Hilarius* means "cheerful" or "happy". In English it is Hilary.

## Hippolytus

The Greek name Hippolytus (Ἱππόλυτος) means "releaser of horses".

## Ignatius

The Greek name *Ignátios ho Theophóros* (Ἰγνάτιος ὁ Θεοφόρος) means "the God-bearing". The shortened Latinized version is Ignatius.

## Irenaeus

The Greek *Eirēnaîos* (Εἰρηναῖος) means "peaceable". The Latinized version is Irenaeus.

## Isaac

The Hebrew name *Yiṣḥāq* (יצחק) means "he laughs". In Greek transliteration is *Isaák* (Ἰσαάκ). The Latinized version is Isaac.

## Isidore

The Greek name *Isídōros* (Ἰσίδωρος) means "gift of Isis", referring to the Egyptian goddess. In Latin it is *Isidorus*. In English it is Isidore.

## Jacob

In Hebrew, the name *Yaʿăqōḇ* (יַעֲקֹב) means "he who takes by the heel" or "supplanter" (Gen. 25:26). The Greek transliteration of this name is *Iákōbos* (Ἰάκωβος). The "os" was added to make the name masculine. The Germanic version is *Jacob* ("J" is pronounced like "Y"). Thus, in English it became Jacob.

## James

The name James is derived from the name Jacob. In Hebrew, the name *Yaʿăqōḇ* (יַעֲקֹב) means "he who takes by the heel" or "supplanter" (Gen. 25:26). The Greek transliteration of this name is *Iákōbos* (Ἰάκωβος). The "os" was added to make the name masculine. The Latinized version is *Iācōbus*. The Germanic version is *Jacoumus* ("J" is pronounced like "Y"). In French, it became *Jammes*. Thus, in English it became James.

## Jerome

The Greek name *Hierṓnumos* (Ἱερώνυμος) means "holy name". The Latinized version is *Hierōnymus*. In French it became *Jérôme*. Thus, in English it is Jerome.

## Jesus

The Hebrew name *Yəhōshúa* (יְהוֹשֻׁעַ) means "the Lord Saves". Its short form is *Yēshū́aʿ* (יֵשׁוּעַ). In Greek, there is no "sh" sound, and the name had to end in "s" to be masculine. Thus, it became *Iēsoûs* (Ἰησοῦς). The Latinized version is *Iesus*. The Germanic version is *Jesus* ("J" pronounced like "Y"). Thus, in English it is Jesus.

## Joachim

The Hebrew name *Yehoyaqim* (יְהוֹיָקִים) means "may the Lord raise up". In Greek it is *Ioakeim* (Ἰωακίμ). In Latin it is *Ioachimus*. The Germanic version is *Joachim* ("J" is pronounced like "Y"). In English it is Joachim.

## John

The Hebrew name *Yôḥānān* (יוֹחָנָן) means "the Lord is gracious". The Greek transliteration of the name is *Iōánnēs* (Ἰωάννης). The "es" was added to make it nominative. The Latinized version is *Iōhannēs*. The Germanic version is *Johannes* ("J" is pronounced like "Y"). In English it became John.

## Jonah

The Hebrew name *Yoná* (יוֹנָה) means "dove". The Germanic version is *Jona* ("J" is pronounced like "Y"). In English it is Jonah.

## Jonathan

The Hebrew name *Yehonatán* (יְהוֹנָתָן) means "the Lord has given". The Germanic version is *Jonathan* ("J" is pronounced like "Y"). In English this became Jonathan.

## Joseph

The Hebrew name *Yoséf* (יוֹסֵף) means "god will Give". The Germanic version is *Josef* ("J" is pronounced like "Y"). In English it is Joseph.

## Judah

The Hebrew name *Y'hudá* (יְהוּדָה) means "praise". The Greek transliteration is *Ioúdas* (Ἰούδας). The "as" was added to make it masculine. The Latinized version is *Iudas*. The Germanic version is *Judas* ("J" is pronounced like "Y"). In English it is Judas, but is also transliterated as Judah. The short form is Jude.

## Justin

The Latin name *Iūstīnus* means "just", "fair", or "righteous". The Greek version is *Ioustînos* (Ἰουστῖνος). The Germanic version is *Justin* ("J" is pronounced like "Y"). In English it is Justin.

## Lazarus

The Hebrew name *'El'azár* (אֶלְעָזָר) or "Eleazar" means "God has helped". In Greek it became *Lā́zāros* (Λᾱ́ζᾱρος). The Latinized version is Lazarus.

## Leo

The Latin name Leo means "lion".

## Levi

The name Levi (לֵוִי) is Hebrew for "attached".

## Luke

The Latin name *Lūcās* means "the bright one". In Greek it is *Loukâs* (Λουκᾶς). In English it is Luke.

## Mark

The Latin name *Mārcus* is derived from Mars, the Roman god of war. In English it is Mark.

## Martha

The Aramaic name *Mārtā* (מָרְתָא) means "matriarch". In English it is Martha.

## Mary

The Hebrew name *Miryām* (מִרְיָם) most likely comes from the Egyptian word *mry* which means "beloved", which in Hebrew sounds like the word for "rebellious". This is fitting as Miriam was a beloved prophetess (Ex. 15:20) who at one time was rebellious toward Moses (Num. 12:1-2). In Greek it is *María* (Μᾰρῐ́ᾱ). In French it is *Marie*. In English it is Mary.

## Matthew

The Hebrew name *Mattiṯyāhū* (מַתִּתְיָהוּ) means "gift of the Lord". In Greek it is *Matthaîos* (Ματθαῖος). The Latinized version is Matthias. In French it is *Mathieu*, thus in English it is Matthew.

## Maximus

The Greek name *Maximos* (Μάξιμος) means "greatest" or "largest". The Latinized version is Maximus.

## Michael

The Hebrew name *Mîḵāʾēl* (מִיכָאֵל) means "who is like God?" In English it is Michael.

## Nathan

The Hebrew name *Natán* (נָתָן) means "gave". In English it is Nathan.

## Nathanael

The Hebrew name *Netan'el* (נְתַנְאֵל) means "God has given". In English it is Nathanael.

## Nicholas

The Greek name *Nikólaos* (Νικόλαος) means "victory of the people". The Latinized version is *Nīcolāus*. In French it is Nicholas, which is also the name we use in English.

## Origen

The Greek name *Ōrigénēs* (Ὠριγένης) means "born of Horus", an Egyptian god in the form of a falcon. In English it is Origen.

## Pachomius

The Coptic name *Paxōm* (Ⲡⲁϧⲱⲙ) means "the falcon". The Greek translation is *Pakhómios* (Παχώμιος), which sounds like "broad-shouldered". The Latinized version is Pachomius.

## Papias

The Greek name *Papias* (Παπίας) means "the guide".

## Patrick

The Latin name *Patricius* means "nobleman". In English it is Patrick.

## Paul

The Latin name *Paulus* means "small". In English it is Paul.

## Peter

In Aramaic, *Kēp̄ā* (כֵּיפָא) means "rock". In Greek, this became *Kēphâs* (Κηφᾶς). The Latinized version is Cephas, which is also the name we use in English. The Greek word for "rock" is *Pétros* (Πέτρος). The Latinized version is *Petrus*. The French word for "rock" is *Pierre*, thus in English it became Peter.

## Philip

The Greek name *Phílippos* (Φίλιππος) means "fond of horses". The Latinized version is *Philippus*. In English it is Philip.

## Polycarp

The Greek name *Polýkarpos* (Πολύκαρπος) means "fruitful". The Latinized version is *Polycarpus*. In English it is Polycarp.

## Quadratus

The Greek name *Kodrâtos* (Κοδρᾶτος) means "square", which implies "proportionate" or "well-made". The Latinized version is Quadratus.

## Saul

The Hebrew name *Shaúl* (שָׁאוּל) means "asked for". The Latinized version is Saul.

## Silas

The Greek name Silas (Σίλας) comes from the Hebrew name *Shaúl* (שָׁאוּל), which means "asked for". In Latin is it Silvanus.

## Simeon

The name *Shimʿôn* (שִׁמְעוֹן) is Hebrew for "hearing" (Deut. 6:4). The Greek transliteration is *Sumeṓn* (Σῠμεών). The Latinized version is Simeon, which is the same name we use in English. It is also sometimes written as Simon.

## Sophronius

The Greek name *Sophrónios* (Σωφρόνιος) is derived from the Greek *sophron* (σώφρων), meaning "self-controlled" or "sensible". The Latinized version is Sophronius.

## Stephen

The Greek name *Stéphanos* (Στέφανος) means "crown". The Latinized version is *Stephanus*. In English it is Stephen.

## Tertullian

The Latin name *Tertullianus* is likely derived from the Latin *tertullus*, meaning "third" or "third-born". In English it is Tertullian.

## Thaddaeus

The Greek name *Thaddaîos* (Θαδδαῖος) comes from the Aramaic word *Taddai* (תדי) meaning "heart". The Latinized version of his name is Thaddaeus, which is the same name we use in English.

## Thomas

The Aramaic name *Tóma* (תאמא) means "twin". The Greek transliteration of this name is *Thōmâs* (Θωμᾶς). The "s" had to be added to make it masculine. In English it is Thomas. The Greek word for "twin" is *Dídumos* (δίδυμος), and the Latinized version is Didymus.

## Timothy

The Gerek name *Tīmótheos* (Τῑμόθεος) means "one who honors God". The Latinized version is *Tīmotheus*. In French it became *Timothée*. In English it is Timothy.

## Titus

The Latin name Titus means "honorable" or "strong".

## Valentine

The Latin name *Valentinus* means "strong" or "powerful". In English it is Valentine.

## William

The name William derives from the Germanic *Wiljahelmaz*, whose roots *wiljô* ("will" or "desire") and *helmaz* ("helmet" or "protection") combine to mean "resolute protector". The Latinized version is *Gulielmus*, from which the Spanish name *Guillermo* is derived. In German it became *Wilhelm*. In English it became William.
