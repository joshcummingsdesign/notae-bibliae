<Initial text="Latin" />

<p id="ad-orientem">Ad orientem – "to the east"</p>

<p id="adventus">Adventus – "arrival" or "approach"</p>

<p id="adversus-haereses">Adversus Haeresēs – "Against Heresies"</p>

<p id="angelus">Angelus – "messenger"</p>

<p id="angelicum">Angelicum – "angelic"</p>

<p id="agnus-dei">Agnus Deī – "Lamb of God", as in Jesus Christ</p>

<p id="alleluia">Allēlūia – from the Hebrew "halleluyá"</p>

<p id="amictus">Amictus – "covered"</p>

<p id="anglicanus">Anglicānus – "adherent of the Church of England"</p>

<p id="antiphonale-romanum">Antiphonalē Rōmanum – "Roman Antiphonal", as in a book of chants for the Roman Rite</p>

<p id="ave-maria">Avē Maria – "Hail Mary"</p>

<p id="beatitudo">Beātitūdō – "blessedness"</p>

<p id="benedicamus-domino">Benedīcāmus Dominō – "Let us bless the Lord"</p>

<p id="benedictio">Benedictiō – "blessing"</p>

<p id="benedictus">Benedictus – "blessed"</p>

<p id="biblia-vulgata">Biblia Vulgāta – "Bible in common tongue"</p>

<p id="breviarium">Breviārium – "summary"</p>

<p id="caesura">Caesūra – "pause in a verse"</p>

<p id="calvaria">Calvāria – "skull", as in a translation of the Aramaic word "Golgotha", the place where Jesus was crucified</p>

<p id="canticulum">Canticulum – "short song"</p>

<p id="cantor">Cantor – "singer", as in the person leading the congregation in singing and prayer</p>

<p id="cantus-selecti">Cantus Sēlectī – "Selected Songs", a book of Gregorian chant compiled by the French Benedictine monks of Solesmes Abbey</p>

<p id="casula">Casula – "little house"</p>

<p id="catacumbae">Catacumbae – "underground burial chambers"</p>

<p id="cinctura">Cinctūra – "belt"</p>

<p id="clavis">Clāvis – "key"</p>

<p id="colloquium">Colloquium – "conversation"</p>

<p id="commendatio">Commendātiō – "a commendation ceremony"</p>

<p id="compnedium-theologiae">Compendium theologiae – "summary of theology"</p>

<p id="completa">Complēta – "completed", as in "complēta hōra", or the last hour of the day</p>

<p id="concordia">Concordia – "an agreeing together"</p>

<p id="confiteor">Cōnfiteor – "I confess", as in the prayer in which we confess we have sinned in thought, word, and deed</p>

<p id="credo">Crēdō – "I believe"</p>

<p id="de-trinitate">Dē trīnitāte – "On the Trinity"</p>

<p id="decadem">Decādem – "set of ten", as in a set of ten beads or Hail Marys in the Rosary</p>

<p id="diademata">Diadēmata – "diadems", or "crowns"</p>

<p id="dieta">Diēta – "meeting of counselors"</p>

<p id="dioecesis">Dioecēsis – "district under a governor", from the Greek "dioíkēsis"</p>

<p id="doctor">Doctor – "teacher"</p>

<p id="dominica">Dominica – "the Lord's Day"</p>

<p id="ex-nihilo">Ex nihilō – "from nothing"</p>

<p id="ex-opere-operato">Ex opere operātō – "from the work worked", referring to sacraments deriving their power from Christ's work rather than the role of humans, and thus not requiring faith to be effective.</p>

<p id="exalto">Exaltō – "to honor" or "to elevate"</p>

<p id="exsulto">Exsultō – "to rejoice", from "saltō"</p>

<p id="extollo">Extollō – "to praise" or "to lift up"</p>

<p id="figura">Figūra – "figure"</p>

<p id="filioque">Fīliōque – "and the Son", as in the Holy Spirit proceeds from the Father and the Son</p>

<p id="frater">Frāter – "brother"</p>

<p id="gaudete">Gaudēte – "rejoice!" or "be joyful!"</p>

<p id="genuflecto">Genūflectō – "to bend the knee"</p>

<p id="gentes">Gentēs – "the nations"</p>

<p id="gloria-in-excelsis-deo">Glōria in excelsīs Deō – "Glory to God in the highest", from Luke 2:14</p>

<p id="gloria-patri">Glōria Patrī – "Glory to the Father"</p>

<p id="graduale-romanum">Gradualē Rōmānum – "Roman Gradual", as in the gradual, or the chant sung after the reading of the Episle, a book of chants for the Roman Rite</p>

<p id="gradualis">Graduālis – "step-by-step", "pertaining to a step", or "gradual" from "gradus"</p>

<p id="gradus">Gradus – "step"</p>

<p id="hosanna-latin">Hosanna – "O save", from the Hebrew "hoshía ná"</p>

<p id="incipit">Incipit – "it begins"</p>

<p id="iniquitas">Inīquitās – "injustice", see the Hebrew "avón"</p>

<p id="introitus">Introitus – "entrance"</p>

<p id="invitatorium">Invītātōrium – "invitation"</p>

<p id="kyriale">Kyrialē – "pertaining to the Kryie"</p>

<p id="laetare">Laetāre – "rejoice!" or "be happy!"</p>

<p id="lapsi">Lāpsī – "fallen"</p>

<p id="laudate">Laudāte – "praise ye!"</p>

<p id="laudo">Laudō – "to praise" or "to extol"</p>

<p id="lectio-divina">Lēctiō Divīna – "Divine Reading", a traditional monastic practice of scriptural reading, meditation, and prayer intended to promote communion with God and to increase the knowledge of God's word</p>

<p id="leviticus">Lēvīticus – "Matters of the Levites", from the Greek "Leuitikón"</p>

<p id="liber-pontificalis">Lībēr Pontificālis – "Pontifical Book"</p>

<p id="liber-usualis">Līber Ūsuālis – short for "Liber Usualis missae et officii pro Dominicis et festis cum cantu Gregoriano" (Book for Use at Masses and Offices of Sundays and Feasts with their Gregorian Chants), a book of Gregorian chant compiled by the French Benedictine monks of Solesmes Abbey</p>

<p id="magnificat">Magnificat – "to magnify", as in to praise, glorify, or extol</p>

<p id="mandatum">Mandātum – "mandate" or "commandment"</p>

<p id="matutinus">Mātūtīnus – "morning"</p>

<p id="mendicans">Mendīcāns – "begging"</p>

<p id="missa">Missa – "Mass", from "missiō"</p>

<p id="missale">Missāle – "pertaining to the Mass"</p>

<p id="missio">Missiō – "a sending", as in a sending forth of the faithful</p>

<p id="nocturnale-romanum">Nocturnāle Rōmānum – "Roman Nocturnal", as in a "nocturne", or a series of prayers for Matins; it is an antiphonal, or book of chants, by German Gregorian Chant scholar Holger Peter Sandhofe</p>

<p id="nocturnus">Nocturnus – "of the night"</p>

<p id="nona">Nōna – "ninth", as in the ninth hour</p>

<p id="nunc-dimittis">Nunc dīmittis – "Now thou doest dismiss", the first words of the Gospel canticle from Luke 2:29-32, Simeon's blessing to the baby Jesus when he was presented at the temple in Jerusalem</p>

<p id="offertorium">Offertōrium – "place where offerings are brought"</p>

<p id="officium">Officium – "a ceremonial duty or service"</p>

<p id="opus-dei">Opus Deī – "Work of God"</p>

<p id="ora-et-labora">Ōrā et laborā – "pray and work"</p>

<p id="orans">Ōrāns – "praying"</p>

<p id="ordinarius">Ōrdinārius – "regular", "ordinary", "orderly", or "pertaining to order"</p>

<p id="ordo-praedicatorum">Ōrdō Praedicatōrum – "Order of Preachers", often abbreviated OP, commonly known as the Dominican Order</p>

<p id="oriens">Oriēns – "daybreak", "dawn", "sunrise", "morning star", or "east"</p>

<p id="papa">Pāpa – "father"</p>

<p id="pascha">Pascha – "the Passover", from the Hebrew "pésach"</p>

<p id="pascha-nostrum">Pascha nostrum – "[Christ] our Passover", a series of antiphons chanted during Eastertide</p>

<p id="paschalis">Paschālis – "pertaining to the Passover", from the Hebrew "pésach"</p>

<p id="passio">Passiō – "suffering" or "enduring"</p>

<p id="pastor">Pāstor – "shepherd"</p>

<p id="pater-noster">Pater Noster – "Our Father", as in the Lord's Prayer</p>

<p id="pax">Pāx – "peace"</p>

<p id="preces">Precēs – "prayers"</p>

<p id="prima">Prīma – "first", as in the first hour</p>

<p id="proslogium">Proslogium – "discourse"</p>

<p id="prosterno">Prōsternō – "to throw to the ground"</p>

<p id="quicunque-vult">Quīcunque vult – "Whoever wants", as in the first words of the Athanasian Creed</p>

<p id="radix">Rādīx – "root"</p>

<p id="rapio">Rapiō – "to carry off"</p>

<p id="raptura">Raptūra – "about to be carried off", from "rapiō"</p>

<p id="rector">Rēctor – "one who steers, guides, or rules"</p>

<p id="reverendus">Reverendus – "one who is to be respected"</p>

<p id="rex">Rēx – "king"</p>

<p id="rosarium">Rosārium – "crown of roses"</p>

<p id="sabbatum">Sabbatum – "the Sabbath"</p>

<p id="sacer">Sacer – "sacred" or "holy"</p>

<p id="sacramentum">Sacrāmentum – "mystery", "oath", "pledge", or "sacrament", from "sacer"</p>

<p id="salto">Saltō – "to jump"</p>

<p id="salve-regina">Salvē Rēgīna – "Hail Queen", a Marian anitphon</p>

<p id="sanctus">Sānctus – "holy"</p>

<p id="sapientia">Sapientia – "wisdom"</p>

<p id="sententia">Sententia – "opinion", "judgment", or "doctrine"</p>

<p id="septuaginta">Septuāgintā – "seventy", LXX</p>

<p id="sepulcrum">Sepulcrum – "tomb", "grave", or "burial place"</p>

<p id="sequentia">Sequentia – "a following", as in a chant or hymn that follows the Alleluia</p>

<p id="sermo">Sermō – "discussion", "conversation", or "discourse"</p>

<p id="servus-servorum-dei">Servūs servōrum Deī – "Servant of the servants of God", a papal title</p>

<p id="sexta">Sexta – "sixth" as in the sixth hour</p>

<p id="schola-cantorum">Schola cantōrum – "school of singers", a trained choir specializing in Gregorian chant</p>

<p id="sola-fide">Sōlā fide – "by faith alone", as in justification by faith</p>

<p id="studium">Studium – "school"</p>

<p id="studium-conventuale">Studium conventuale – "conventual school", medieval House of Studies of the Dominical Order in Rome</p>

<p id="studium-generale">Studium generale – "general school", medieval House of Studies</p>

<p id="summa-theologiae">Summā Theolōgiae – "Summary of Theology"</p>

<p id="symbolum-apostolorum">Symbolum Apostolōrum – "Apostles' Creed"</p>

<p id="te-decet-laus">Tē decet laus – "to you praise is due"</p>

<p id="tertia">Tertia – "third", as in the third hour</p>

<p id="textus-receptus">Textus Receptus – "Received Text"</p>

<p id="toni-communes">Tonī commūnēs – "common tones", as in the standard melodic formulas in Gregorian chant</p>

<p id="tonus-peregrinus">Tonus peregrīnus – "wandering tone", a chant tone which "wanders" from the reciting tone, i.e. the reciting note changes at the Mediation</p>

<p id="tractus">Tractus – "drawn out" or "prolonged"</p>

<p id="transfiguratio">Transfigūrātio – "a major change in appearance or form"</p>

<p id="transgredior">Trānsgredior – "to go beyond", as in a set boundary, see the Hebrew "pésha"</p>

<p id="transubstantiatio">Trānsubstantiātiō – "conversion of one substance into another"</p>

<p id="tridentinus">Tridentīnus – "pertaining to [the Council of] Trent"</p>

<p id="triduum">Trīduum – "three days"</p>

<p id="trinitas">Trīnitās – "Trinity"</p>

<p id="tunica-alba">Tunica alba – "white tunic"</p>

<p id="venite-exultemus-domino">Venīte exultēmus Dominō – "Come let us praise the Lord", from Psalm 95:1</p>

<p id="veritas">Vēritās – "truth"</p>

<p id="versiculus">Versiculus – "short verse"</p>

<p id="vesperus">Vesperus – "evening"</p>

<p id="vetus-latina">Vetus Latīna – "Old Latin"</p>

<p id="vigilia">Vigilia – "watching" or "wakefulness"</p>
