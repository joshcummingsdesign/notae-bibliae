## Introducing the Passage

The week of Jesus' Triumphal Entry into Jerusalem (Matt. 21:10), he spends time teaching in the temple (Matt. 21:23). Exasperated with the Scribes and the Pharisees, he finally delivers seven woes to them (Matt. 23:13), and begins to lament over Jerusalem.

**"O Jerusalem, Jerusalem, the one who kills the prophets and stones those who are sent to her! How often I wanted to gather your children together, as a hen gathers her chicks under her wings, but you were not willing! See! Your house is left to you desolate; for I say to you, you shall see me no more till you say, 'Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord!'"** (Matt. 23:37-39).

Then Jesus leaves the temple, and as he is leaving, the apostles point out to him the beauty of the temple, as if to intercede for the holy city (Matt. 24:1; Mark 13:1; Luke 21:5).

And Jesus says to them, **"Do you not see all these things? Assuredly, I say to you, not one stone shall be left here upon another, that shall not be thrown down"** (Matt. 24:2; Mark 13:2; Luke 21:6).

Then he leads the apostles east to the Mount of Olives, and they ask him, *"Tell us, when will these things be? And what will be the sign of your Coming, and of the End of the Age?"* (Matt. 24:3; Mark 13:4; Luke 21:7).

Their question is twofold:

1. When will "these things" happen, that is, when will the temple be destroyed?
2. What will be the sign of the <Definition lang="english" anchor="second-coming" text="Second Coming" /> and of the <Definition lang="english" anchor="end-of-the-age" text="End of the Age" />?

## 1. The Destruction of the Temple
Jesus begins by addressing the first part of the question: the destruction of the temple.

First, he gives them a warning about being led astray during the time of calamity leading up to the temple's destruction, saying, **"Take heed that you not be deceived. For many will come in my name, saying, 'I am [the Christ],' and, 'The time has drawn near.' Therefore do not go after them"** (Matt. 24:4-5; Mark 13:5-6; Luke 21:8). For false prophets know that any time of calamity is an opportune one to draw attention unto themselves.

Then he tells them not to be alarmed and to remain patient even when they see the preliminary signs of the temple's destruction drawing near, saying, **"And you will hear of wars and rumors of wars. See that you are not troubled; for all these things must come to pass, but the end is not yet"** (Matt. 24:6; Mark 13:7; Luke 21:9). For man's tendency is to panic when signs of danger are approaching. Thus, Jesus bids them hold fast in the faith.

Then he tells them that the signs leading up to the destruction of the temple are not signs of the End of the Age, but rather, a foretaste of the <Definition lang="english" anchor="great-tribulation" text="Great Tribulation" />, which comes later, saying, **"For nation will rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom. And there will be famines, pestilences, and earthquakes in various places. All these are the beginning of sorrows"** (Matt. 24: 7-8; Mark 13:8; Luke 21:10-11). Luke adds "great signs from heaven," to point out that these events are divinely ordained.[^1]

Then the Lord tells the apostles what they will be called to do before the temple's destruction, saying, **"But watch out for yourselves, for they will deliver you up to councils, and you will be beaten in the synagogues. You will be brought before rulers and kings for my sake, for a testimony to them. And the gospel must first be preached to all the nations. But when they arrest you and deliver you up, do not worry beforehand, or premeditate what you will speak. But whatever is given you in that hour, speak that; for it is not you who speak, but the Holy Spirit. Now brother will betray brother to death, and a father his child; and children will rise up against parents and cause them to be put to death. And you will be hated by all for my name's sake. But he who endures to the end shall be saved"** (Matt. 24:9-14; Mark 13:9-13, Luke 21:12-19). And indeed through the apostles, the gospel went out, and they suffered for the faith at the hands of brutal men, but they remained steadfast, preaching the word of the Lord and saving many. Matthew adds, "and then the end will come," that is, the destruction of the temple.[^2]

Then the Lord tells them what the ultimate sign of the destruction of the temple will be, saying, **"Therefore when you see the 'Abomination of Desolation,' spoken of by Daniel the prophet, standing in the holy place (whoever reads, let him understand), then let those who are in Judea flee to the mountains"** (Matt. 24:15-16; Mark 13:14, Luke 21:20-21). Luke expressly states, "But when you see Jerusalem surrounded by armies, then know that its desolation is near." Now, it is written, "Whoever reads, let him understand," thus, let us read the passage in question.

**Daniel 9:24-27**<br/>
Daniel is told by the Archangel Gabriel, **"Seventy weeks are determined for your people and for your holy city,"** that is, 70 weeks of years or 70 x 7 = 490 years  (cf. Lev. 25:8), **"to finish the transgression, to make an end of sins, to make reconciliation for iniquity, to bring in everlasting righteousness, to seal up vision and prophecy, and to anoint the Most Holy."** Christ, the Most Holy Anointed One, will fulfill the Law and the Prophets (Matt. 5:17), make an end of sins by nailing them to the cross (Col. 2:13-15), and bring in everlasting righteousness by making us servants of God who bear fruit unto sanctification and its end, everlasting life (Rom. 6:22).[^3] **"Know therefore and understand, that from the going forth of the command to restore and build Jerusalem,"** that is, in the 20th year of Artaxerxes, King of the Persians (c. 455 BC); for it was his cupbearer, Nehemiah, who petitioned the king and obtained his request that Jerusalem be rebuilt (Neh. 2:1-5),[^4] **"until Messiah the Prince, there shall be seven weeks and sixty-two weeks;"** that is, 69 x 7 = 483 years, which brings us to the beginning of Jesus' ministry (c. 29 AD) during the reign of Herod the tetrarch (Matt. 14:1), **"the street shall be built again, and the wall, even in troublesome times."** For God protected Nehemiah and his men when their enemies came upon them, and they finished building the wall of the temple (Neh. 4:7-9), **"And after the sixty-two weeks Messiah shall be cut off,"** that is, in the 70th week, Christ shall suffer and die (Matt. 27:1), **"but not for himself;"** for the sins of the world (Isa. 53:5) (John 1:29), **"and the people of the prince who is to come shall destroy the city and the sanctuary."** Titus, the Roman general, and his men shall come and besiege Jerusalem, destroying the temple (70 AD). **"The end of it shall be with a flood, and till the end of the war desolations are determined."** The Hebrew for "flood" is *<Definition lang="hebrew" anchor="shetef" text="shetef" />*, which the Vulgate renders as *<Definition lang="latin" anchor="vastitas" text="vastitas" />* (devastation), conveying the sense that the temple will be laid waste. **"Then he shall confirm a covenant with many for one week;"** the covenant in Christ's blood (Matt. 26:28), which is confirmed in the 70th week,[^5] **"but in the middle of the week he shall bring an end to sacrifice and offering."** For Christ's three-and-a-half-year ministry on earth came to a close halfway through the 70th week (c. 33 AD) when his Passion put an end to the sacrifices of old (Heb. 7:26-28).[^6] **"And on the wing of abominations,"** that is, the pinnacle, as the Hebrew *<Definition lang="hebrew" anchor="kenaf" text="kenaf" />* can mean "wing" or "extremity", denoting the furthest point, **"shall be one who makes desolate,"** that is, Titus, who serves as a type—but not the fulfillment—of the <Definition lang="english" anchor="the-antichrist" text="Antichrist" /> (1 John 4:3), who in the end will seat himself in the temple of God, and make himself out to be as God (2 Thess. 2:3-4),[^7] **"even until the consummation, which is determined, is poured out on the desolate."** For the Antichrist will continue to exalt himself until the End of the Age (2 Thess. 2:7-8) when he and the devil are cast into the lake of fire (Rev. 19:20) (Rev. 20:10), and all that have done evil shall come forth unto the <Definition lang="english" anchor="resurrection-of-judgment" text="Resurrection of Judgment" /> (John 5:28-29).[^8]

**"Therefore when you see the 'Abomination of Desolation,' spoken of by Daniel the prophet, standing in the holy place,"** says Christ to the apostles, **"then let those who are in Judea flee to the mountains. Let him who is on the housetop not go down to take anything out of his house. And let him who is in the field not go back to get his clothes. But woe to those who are pregnant and to those who are nursing babies in those days! And pray that your flight may not be in winter or on the Sabbath"** (Matt. 24:15-20; Mark 13:14-18, Luke 21:20-22). Luke adds, "For these are the days of vengeance, that all things which are written may be fulfilled," meaning the things written in the Book of Daniel about the destruction of the temple.[^9]

Then the Lord gives the reason for his warning, **"For then there will be great tribulation, such as has not been since the beginning of the world until this time, no, nor ever shall be"** (Matt. 24:21; Mark 13:19; Luke 21:23-24). "Until this time," meaning until the destruction of the temple, which he has just finished explaining.[^10] Luke adds, "And they will fall by the edge of the sword, and be led away captive into all nations. And Jerusalem will be trampled by Gentiles until the times of the Gentiles are fulfilled," that is, until God has gathered his elect from among the Gentiles.[^11] Then the Lord adds, **"And unless those days were shortened, no flesh would be saved; but for the elect's sake those days will be shortened"** (Matt. 24:22; Mark 13:20), which shows his great mercy in not wanting that any of his own should perish (Matt. 18:14).[^12]

## 2. The Second Coming

Next, Jesus addresses the second part of the question: the Second Coming.

First, he gives another warning about being led astray, but this time on account of the falling away that coincides with the fulfillment of the Antichrist (2 Thess. 2:3-4), about whom he has just finished discussing. He tells them, **"Then if anyone says to you, 'Look, here is the Christ!' or, 'Look, he is there!' do not believe it. For false christs and false prophets will rise and show signs and wonders to deceive, if possible, even the elect. But take heed; see, I have told you all things beforehand"** (Matt. 24:23-26; Mark 13:21-23).

Then he gives the reason they ought not be shaken in mind or troubled as though they would miss the day of his return (cf. 2 Thess. 2:1-2): **"For as the lightning comes from the east and flashes to the west, so also will the coming of the Son of Man be"** (Matt. 24:27), that is, Christ will manifest himself in the Second Coming in such a way that it will be easily seen and understood by all.[^13] And he follows this with a saying: **"For wherever the carcass is, there the eagles will be gathered together"** (Matt. 24:28), which, as [St. Thomas Aquinas](/people/saints/medieval/thomas-aquinas) explains, gives the following sense: "It will be like what happened when a man asked his master, who was concealing his plan about moving the camp, many questions, and said, 'When will you move the camp?' And the master said, 'Will you not hear the trumpet? Why are you asking?'"[^14]

Then the Lord tells the apostles the signs of the Second Coming, saying, **"But in those days, after that tribulation,"** that is, after both the destruction of the temple and the eventual fulfillment of Antichrist,[^15] **"the sun will be darkened, and the moon will not give its light; the stars of heaven will fall, and the powers in the heavens will be shaken. Then they will see the Son of Man coming in the clouds with great power and glory. And then he will send his angels, and gather together his elect from the four winds, from the farthest part of earth to the farthest part of heaven"** (Matt. 24:29-31; Mark 13:24-27; Luke 21:25-28). Matthew adds the word "immediately"—that is, immediately after the Great Tribulation—to show us that God's desire is to redeem his people quickly.[^16] Luke adds, "Now when these things begin to happen, look up and lift up your heads, because your redemption draws near," words of comfort indeed.

As for the time of the Second Coming, Jesus expressly states, **"But of that day and hour no one knows, not even the angels in heaven, nor the Son, but only the Father"** (Matt. 24:36; Mark 13:32).
