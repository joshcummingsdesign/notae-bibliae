How do you explain the Trinity? I'll attempt to give you the answer [Dominican](/people/saints/medieval/dominic-of-guzman) friar Fr. Thomas Joseph White once gave. First it can be explained practically, then by analogy, and then philosophically.

First, practically. We come to understand the Trinity through faith in Jesus. We first have to ask ourselves if Jesus' words are true, if he was raised from the dead, if he is the Son of God, and so forth (John 8:58); and when we come to believe, we begin to understand what it means when Jesus speaks of the Father—who is God, yet distinct from Jesus (John 17:20-21)—and the Holy Spirit—who is God, yet distinct from Jesus and the Father (John 15:26); and we come to understand how the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit are all distinct Persons, yet the one God (Deut. 6:4).[^1]

Then we come up with analogies. One of the most famous being the Psychological Analogy, articulated by [St. Augustine](/people/saints/latin-fathers/augustine-of-hippo). We are made in the image of God, and so within us there is a kind of reflection of the Trinity. Our mind can know itself, and in knowing itself can love itself; and just as mind, knowledge, and love are three realities yet once essence—that is, they are spiritual or incorporeal—so God is Word (Knowledge) (John 1:1) and Spirit (Love) (1 John 4:8).[^2]

Another analogy is the communion of three persons who have one nature. Just as three of us share the same nature (humanity), the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit share the same nature (<Definition lang="english" anchor="godhead" text="Godhead" />), yet they are one in being.

Then comes the more philosophical explanation. In nature we see beings (things that exist), but not every being is a person. A rock, for example, is a being of zero persons; you are a being of one person; and God is a being of three Persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

As Christians, we believe that God created the world for the communion of persons, and that in God primally is a communion of Persons. We worship one God in Trinity, and Trinity in Unity; neither confounding the Persons, nor dividing the Substance.[^3] When you come to know Jesus, you come to know the Trinity; and God is waiting for you with open arms to get to know him.
