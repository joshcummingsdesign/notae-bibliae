## The First Resurrection

In the beginning, God made man and breathed into him the breath of life. All that was needed for his flourishing was provided, he was given abundance and dominion, and he was in the presence of his gracious Creator and the tree of life.

But, deceived by that ancient serpent, the devil, man turned away from God, and apart from God became enslaved to his passions. Thus, God numbered his days. And so through sin came death.

But to redeem us from our sins, God sent his Son, Jesus Christ, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have everlasting life. For the Lord himself tells us that through faith, we pass from death to life, saying, **"Most assuredly, I say to you, he who hears my word and believes in him who sent me has everlasting life, and shall not come into judgment, but has passed from death into life"** (John 5:24).

And before he ascended into heaven, he commissioned the apostles, saying, **"All authority has been given to me in heaven and on earth. Go therefore and make disciples of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all things that I have commanded you; and lo, I am with you always, even to the End of the Age"** (Matt. 28:18-20).

It is the passing from death to life through Baptism that we call the [**First Resurrection**](/glossary/theological-terms#first-resurrection), that is, the resurrection of the soul.[^1] As it is written, **"Blessed and holy is he who has part in the First Resurrection"** (Rev. 20:6). Thus the Apostle tells us, **"We were buried with him through Baptism into death, that just as Christ was raised from the dead by the glory of the Father, even so we also should walk in newness of life"** (Rom. 6:4).

## The Second Coming

Having thus passed from death to life, the Christian lives in the quiet and patient expectation of the return of his Redeemer. For the Lord himself says, **"They will see the Son of Man coming in a cloud with power and great glory. Now when these things begin to happen, look up and lift up your heads, because your redemption draws near"** (Luke 21:27-28). It is the return of Christ that we call the [**Second Coming**](/glossary/theological-terms#second-coming), and from it we may understand the other events which are to take place at the end of salvation history.

## This Age and The Age to Come

For it is from the Second Coming that we understand the meaning of "This Age", "the End of the Age", and "the Age to Come". As the Lord says, **"Assuredly, I say to you, there is no one who has left house or parents or brothers or wife or children, for the sake of the kingdom of God, who shall not receive many times more in This Age, and in the Age to Come eternal life"** (Luke 18:29-39). And elsewhere, **"The kingdom of heaven is like a dragnet that was cast into the sea and gathered some of every kind, which, when it was full, they drew to shore; and they sat down and gathered the good into vessels, but threw the bad away. So it will be at the End of the Age"** (Matt. 13:47-49).

[**This Age**](/glossary/theological-terms#this-age) refers to the present time, that is, the time before the Second Coming. The Second Coming marks the [**End of the Age**](/glossary/theological-terms#end-of-the-age), which ushers in the [**Age to Come**](/glossary/theological-terms#age-to-come). Notice, however, that Our Lord uses the present tense when he speaks of the kingdom of heaven and of eternal life, saying, **"Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand!"** (Matt. 3:2), and **"Most assuredly, I say to you, he who believes in me has everlasting life"** (John 6:47), signifying that these are present realities, though they will be realized in all their fullness in the Age to Come.

## Death

But what happens when we die a bodily death? Scripture tells us that when we die, **"the dust will return to the earth as it was, and the spirit will return to God who gave it"** (Eccl. 12:7). Hence we read that when the Lord died a bodily death, he "yielded up his spirit" (Matt. 27:50), that is, the breath of life. In this way, Scripture describes death as the separation of body and soul. Thus the Apostle writes, **"We are confident, yes, well pleased rather to be absent from the body and to be present with the Lord"** (2 Cor. 5:8).

But the soul only finds rest in the Word of God. Thus, those who rest in the Word of God remain in that rest when they die. And who rested more fully in the Word of God than Abraham? Therefore the Lord says, **"So it was that the beggar died, and was carried by the angels to Abraham’s bosom"** (Luke 16:22), that is, to the comfort of God's promises in Christ (cf. Gen. 22:18). And in that comfort the faithful rest until the End of the Age. Thus, when Lazarus yielded up his spirit, Christ says, **“Our friend Lazarus sleeps, but I go that I may wake him up”** (John 11:11). For death is mere sleep in the eyes of the Lord when a soul awaits the resurrection of the body.

But it follows that the soul which does not rest in the Word of God finds only torment and despair. Thus the Lord says, **"The rich man also died and was buried. And being in torments in Hades, he lifted up his eyes and saw Abraham afar off"** (Luke 16:22-23). And between this rest and despair there is a great chasm fixed, which cannot be crossed (Luke 16:26). And in this state the soul remains until the End of the Age.[^2]

## The Devil is Bound

But who held the power of death but the devil, who wielded it over us like a most terrible sword, accusing us day and night? Thus Christ our Savior came and bound the devil by his life, death, and resurrection, that we may reign with him—our High Priest, who is seated at the right hand of the God the Father Almighty—unto the End of the Age. Thus it is written, **"I saw an angel coming down from heaven, having the key to the bottomless pit and a great chain in his hand. He laid hold of the dragon, that serpent of old, who is the devil and Satan, and bound him for a thousand years"** (Rev. 20:1-2), where "a thousand years" refers to the entirety of This Age.[^3] Hence we read, **"The rest of the dead did not live again until the thousand years were finished"** (Rev. 20:5), that is, those who do not share in the First Resurrection remain in a state of spiritual death until the End of the Age.[^4]

## The Destruction of the Second Temple

On the Mount of Olives, after the Lord tells the apostles that the temple will be destroyed, they ask him two questions: **"When will these things be? And what will be the sign of your Coming, and of the End of the Age?"** (Matt. 24:1-3).

The first question is: *When will these things be?*—that is: *When will the temple be destroyed?* To which the Lord replies, **"When you see the 'Abomination of Desolation,' spoken of by Daniel the prophet, standing in the holy place (whoever reads, let him understand), then let those who are in Judea flee to the mountains"** (Matt. 24:15-16). [**"Abomination of Desolation"**](/glossary/theological-terms#abomination-of-desolation) here refers to the desolator who will lead the destruction of the temple (cf. Dan. 9:27)—whom we now know to be the Roman general Titus in 70 AD.[^5] And to clear up any confusion, Luke's Gospel expressly states: **"When you see Jerusalem surrounded by armies, then know that its desolation is near"** (Luke 21:20). Or put another way, "When you see the desolator standing in the temple, then let those who are in Judea flee to the mountains."

## Signs of the Second Coming

The second question is: *What will be the sign of your Coming, and of the End of the Age?*—to which the Lord replies, **"The sun will be darkened, and the moon will not give its light; the stars of heaven will fall, and the powers in the heavens will be shaken. Then they will see the Son of Man coming in the clouds with great power and glory. And then he will send his angels, and gather together his elect from the four winds, from the farthest part of earth to the farthest part of heaven"** (Mark 13:24-27).

In other words, the Second Coming will be unmistakably clear, and we need not fear missing it or being deceived about it.[^6] Thus the Lord precedes this with: **"If they say to you, 'Look, he is in the desert!' do not go out; or 'Look, he is in the inner rooms!' do not believe it. For as the lightning comes from the east and flashes to the west, so also will the Coming of the Son of Man be"** (Matt. 24:26-27). And to dispel any remaining desire or motive on our part to engage in vain speculation about the day or hour of the Second Coming, he adds: **"But of that day and hour no one knows, not even the angels in heaven, nor the Son, but only the Father"** (Mark 13:32).

## The Great Tribulation

Though Christ had thoroughly and sufficiently answered the apostles' question, certain doubts nevertheless remained in the hearts and minds of believers, as was the case for the church at Thessalonica. Thus the Apostle writes, **"Now, brethren, concerning the Coming of our Lord Jesus Christ and our gathering together to him, we ask you, not to be soon shaken in mind or troubled, either by spirit or by word or by letter, as if from us, as though the day of Christ had come. Let no one deceive you by any means"** (2 Thess. 2:1-3).

And to further ease their concerns, he tells them what is to first take place: **"For that Day will not come unless the falling away comes first, and the man of sin is revealed, the son of perdition, who opposes and exalts himself above all that is called God or that is worshiped, so that he sits as God in the temple of God, showing himself that he is God"** (2 Thess. 2:3-4; cf. Dan. 11:36).

First, he says, is the <Definition lang="greek" anchor="apostasia" text='"falling away"' />, to which Christ refers when he says, **"If anyone says to you, 'Look, here is the Christ!' or 'There!' do not believe it. For false christs and false prophets will rise and show great signs and wonders to deceive, if possible, even the elect. See, I have told you beforehand"** (Matt. 24:23-25).[^7] It is of this same falling away that we read, **"Now when the thousand years have expired, Satan will be released from his prison and will go out to deceive the nations which are in the four corners of the earth"** (Rev. 20:7-8). This falling away is referred to as the [**Great Apostasy**](/glossary/theological-terms#great-apostasy).

And with this, he says, comes the emergence of the [**Antichrist**](/glossary/theological-terms#the-antichrist), the "son of perdition" who is the ultimate fulfillment of the Abomination of Desolation, and of whom previous [antichrists](/glossary/theological-terms#an-antichrist) were but prefigurations.[^8][^9] Thus we read, **"Little children, it is the last hour; and as you have heard that the Antichrist is coming, even now many antichrists have come, by which we know that it is the last hour"** (1 John 2:18). It is through the Antichrist that Satan will work to deceive the nations and make war with the saints. For it is written: **"They went up on the breadth of the earth and surrounded the camp of the saints and the beloved city"** (Rev. 20:9), that is, he shall persecute the Church in the final trial on earth before the Second Coming.[^10]

This final trial is referred to as the [**Great Tribulation**](/glossary/theological-terms#great-tribulation). For it is written: **"There shall be a time of trouble, such as never was since there was a nation, even to that time"** (Dan. 12:1). And its duration shall be three-and-a-half years. For we read, **"From the time that the daily sacrifice is taken away, and the Abomination of Desolation is set up, there shall be 1,290 days"** (Dan. 12:11), where "daily sacrifice" refers to the worship of the Church.[^11]

During this time, the Church will persevere in the faith until the End of the Age,[^12] when the Lord will "consume the Lawless One with the breath of his mouth and destroy him with the brightness of his Coming" (2 Thess. 2:8). Hence we read, **"And fire came down from God out of heaven and devoured them"** (Rev. 20:9).[^13]

## The Second Resurrection

After the Great Tribulation and the Second Coming, all shall rise bodily. For the Lord speaking of himself says, **"The hour is coming in which all who are in the graves will hear his voice and come forth—those who have done good, to the Resurrection of Life, and those who have done evil, to the Resurrection of Judgment"** (John 5:28-29; cf. Dan. 12:2), where the [**Resurrection of Life**](/glossary/theological-terms#resurrection-of-life) is the rising unto everlasting life, and where the [**Resurrection of Judgment**](/glossary/theological-terms#resurrection-of-judgment) is the rising unto condemnation. It is this bodily rising at the End of the Age that we call the [**Second Resurrection**](/glossary/theological-terms#second-resurrection).[^14]

But what about the faithful who are found alive at the End of the Age? The Apostle tells us, **"The dead in Christ will rise first. Then we who are alive and remain shall be caught up together with them in the clouds to meet the Lord in the air. And thus we shall always be with the Lord"** (1 Thess. 4:16-17).

## The Final Judgment

But so perfect is the Lord's justice that all must "give an account to God who is ready to judge the living and the dead" (1 Peter 4:5). It is the public manifestation of Christ's justice at the End of the Age that we call the [**Final Judgment**](/glossary/theological-terms#final-judgment). Thus the Apostle says, **"For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ, that each one may receive the things done in the body, according to what he has done, whether good or bad"** (2 Cor. 5:10; cf. Ecclesiastes 12:14).

And at this time, those who are judged to their condemnation will face the [**Second Death**](/glossary/theological-terms#second-death),[^15] that is, damnation—the same fate which awaits that ancient serpent, the devil. For it is written: **"Then Death and Hades were cast into the lake of fire. This is the Second Death. And anyone not found written in the Book of Life was cast into the lake of fire"** (Rev. 20:14-15), where "Death and Hades" refers to the devil and his angles, and where the "Book of Life" refers to God's foreknowledge of his elect.[^16]

But does this mean that those who rose in the First Resurrection should live in fear of damnation? By no means! For it is written: **"Blessed and holy is he who has part in the First Resurrection. Over such the Second Death has no power"** (Rev. 20:6).

So then, how are the redeemed of Christ judged at the End of the Age? By faith working through love. For the Lord himself says, **"Come, you blessed of my Father, inherit the kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world: for I was hungry and you gave me food; I was thirsty and you gave me drink; I was a stranger and you took me in; I was naked and you clothed me; I was sick and you visited me; I was in prison and you came to me"** (Matt. 25:34-36). For in these works of mercy, the love of Christ bears its fruit. And if you trust in Christ, then though you stumble, he is nevertheless at work in you, and the Final Judgment is not terror for you, but vindication.[^17] Thus the Apostle says, **"He who has begun a good work in you will complete it until the day of Jesus Christ"** (Phil. 1:6).

## New Heavens and New Earth

After the Final Judgment, those who share in the Resurrection of Life shall be made incorruptible (1 Cor. 15:53), and the heavens and the earth shall pass away; that is, the whole created order is put through God's purifying fire and renewed into a more glorious state, perfectly suited to the immortal life of the saints.[^18] This renewed creation is referred to as the [**New Heavens and New Earth**](/glossary/theological-terms#new-heavens-and-new-earth) (Rev. 21:1; cf. Isa. 65:17; cf. 2 Peter 3:11-13).

Then the holy and heavenly [**City of God**](/glossary/theological-terms#city-of-god) is consummated, that is, the whole communion of saints and angels shall dwell in the presence of God in eternal blessedness.[^19] As it is written, **"Then I, John, saw the holy city, New Jerusalem, coming down out of heaven from God, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband"** (Rev. 21:2), where "New Jerusalem" refers to the City of God brought to its perfection by grace, which comes from above,[^20] and where "bride" refers to the Church, clothed in righteousness by Christ the Bridegroom (cf. Eph. 5:25-27).

In the presence of God, therefore, the [**Beatific Vision**](/glossary/theological-terms#beatific-vision) is realized, that is, seeing God face to face as he is (1 Cor. 13:12).[^21] It is this eternal state of blessedness that we call [**Heaven**](/glossary/theological-terms#heaven).

**"And God will wipe away every tear from their eyes; there shall be no more death, nor sorrow, nor crying. There shall be no more pain, for the former things have passed away"** (Rev. 21:4). **"They shall see his face ... There shall be no night there: They need no lamp nor light of the sun, for the Lord God gives them light. And they shall reign forever and ever"** (Rev. 22:4-5). Amen.
